Professional education and training

Working with diversity and difference requires a broad knowledge of issues faced by a range of service users. Thatâ€™s why support workers need to continually learn new skills. Participating in ongoing training is especially necessary to keep up-to-date with new laws and regulations.

Professional education and training covers many areas and can be done in many ways.

  1. Informal professional development includes discussions with colleagues, supervisors, and service users. Reflective practice, for example, can be done individually by writing in a journal or by meeting with your supervisors.
  2. Attending professional development days and seminars are more formal methods of learning. Short online courses are also easily accessible forms of learning.
  3. You may also choose to upgrade your qualification by enrolling in a higher level course through TAFE or University.
