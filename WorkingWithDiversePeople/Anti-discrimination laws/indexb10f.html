Protected attributes

The Victorian Equal Opportunity Act 1995 makes it unlawful to discriminate against anyone because of a certain protected characteristic or attribute(s).

Select each of the check boxes to reveal the protected attributes.
  • Age
  • Breastfeeding
  • Gender identity
  • Disability (including discrimination based on having an assistance aid, such as equipment like a wheelchair or cane, an assistance dog or a person providing assistance or services)
  • Employment activity (this means asking your employer about your entitlements or raising a concern about not receiving your entitlements)
  • Industrial activity
  • Lawful sexual activity (e.g., lawfully employed sex worker)
  • Marital status
  • Status as a parent or carer
  • Physical features
  • Pregnancy
  • Race
  • Sex
  • Sexual orientation
  • Political or religious beliefs or activities
  • An expunged homosexual conviction (a person who has successfully applied to have their historic homosexual conviction removed from the record)
  • Personal association with anyone who has any of these characteristics
