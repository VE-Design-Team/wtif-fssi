Direct and indirect discrimination

There are two types of discrimination:

  1. Direct discrimination occurs when someone with an attribute protected under the law is treated negatively because of that attribute. This includes bullying or harassment, humiliation, denial of benefits that are available to others, being provided a benefit on unfavourable terms, unfair allocation of tasks and exclusion from essential communication.
  2. Indirect discrimination isnâ€™t as obvious. It occurs when someone imposes a practice, policy or rule which may appear to treat people equally but disadvantages a group with certain attributes.
