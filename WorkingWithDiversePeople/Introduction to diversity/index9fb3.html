Diversity at work

Australia is made up of many different types of people. As a support worker, you can expect to engage with a range of diverse communities and groups, not just the service users and their families but also your co-workers and management.

Select the images to learn more about the diverse range of communities service workers engage with.

People who identify as Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander (ATSI) who may come from various backgrounds, with a variety of spiritual beliefs, native languages and hair, skin or eye colour. There are more than 250 different Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander language groups in Australia.

Culturally and Linguistically Diverse (CALD) communities contribute to over 300 languages different languages spoken in Australian homes. 

People with a range of disabilities (sensory, visual, auditory, intellectual, physical) and mental health issues, all contribute to our Australian society.

People’s gender identities vary. Sexual preferences are broad. People may identify as lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, intersex and queer (LGBTIQ) or other.

Life expectancy in Australia has increased, leaving a larger aging population either living full and active lives or requiring care as they become physically and mentally frail and dependent on families and society.

Our increasing population through birth rate plus immigration further enrich our cultural diversity.
