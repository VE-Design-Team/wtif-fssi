Effects of discrimination

When a person experiences discrimination, it subjects them to high levels of stress which can lead to further physical and mental health problems such as anxiety, depression, obesity, high blood pressure and substance abuse. When discrimination is part of the system and people expect it, it causes them to avoid situations where they think they will be treated poorly. These people end up missing out on education, work and other opportunities. When an entire community feels discriminated against, it can sometimes lead to violence and crime. It can also make it far more difficult for those groups to lift themselves out of poverty.
