Intersectional framework

The intersectionality framework deals with how privilege and oppression meet and the complex ways people interact in terms of race, class, gender and sexuality. Adopting an intersectional perspective can help support workers navigate service users’ self-imposed identity and overcome the interests of power and privilege. It will help you to recognise that oppressors and victims are not necessarily separate. Some people experience discrimination and oppression even when they are part of a dominant group.  

 

Example

For example, a white (race) woman (gender) from a high socio-economic group (class) may feel superior to others. She may also experience workplace discrimination due to mental illness (disability) and being older (age). Support workers need to appreciate these complexities in order to better understand the individual experience of discrimination, oppression and privilege. This helps to create a workplace that fully embraces and celebrates difference and diversity.
