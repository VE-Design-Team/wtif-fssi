Sexual abuse

Select each of the headings below to learn more about the bodily and behavioural signs of sexual abuse.
  • Bodily Signs
  • Behaviour Signs

Type of injuries specific to sexual abuse:

  • sexually transmitted disease or infections 
  • vaginal or anal bleeding or discharge.

Location of injuries:

  • bite marks or scratches on the face, neck, chest, abdomen, thighs or buttocks
  • bruising in the genital areas, inner thighs, buttocks or around the breasts
  • fingertip bruising on upper arms.

Additional clues:

  • torn, stained, or bloody undergarments, continence aids, or linen
  • difficulty in walking.

Like with physical abuse, the person may avoid or be fearful of certain people or situations, not want to see a doctor or talk about their injuries.

Other behavioural signs include:

  • increased anxiety or other emotional changes
  • use of sexually explicit language or references 
  • changes in sleep patterns, sleep disturbances, recurring nightmares.
