Societal impacts

Why might people with disabilities need support in achieving security, autonomy and influence? It is because they are more likely to experience a poor quality of life. People with disabilities are at greater risk of discrimination and violence, leading to social isolation and exclusion. This means they miss out on learning and work opportunities, which can be made worse by limited access to information and resources. The result is financial stress, inadequate housing and ill health. There are three types of barriers that put people with disabilities at a disadvantage in society:

  1. Attitudinal: negative views and misunderstandings of disability
  2. Environmental: the absence or presence of something in a personâ€™s surroundings that restricts their abilities
  3. Institutional: policies and practices that are unfair to people with disability.
