Active citizenship

Active citizenship is when a person takes part in their community to improve the lives of others. People can be active citizens by giving their time or sharing their expertise and resources with their local, national and international communities.

Select each of the cards to flip and reveal examples of active citizenship.

Local active citizenship: Participating in a local council meeting to discuss cost, safety, location and installation time for a proposed playground in the neighbourhood park.

National active citizenship: Volunteering your time to help pick up rubbish in your neighbourhood during Clean-Up Australia Day.

 

International active citizenship: Donating money to organisations like the Red Cross so they can purchase food, water or medical supplies for areas in need.
