Navigating conflict

In any work environment where people must interact, there is the possibility of conflict. As a support worker, you may find yourself in situations where you disagree with the service user and/or their carers and family. Knowing the service user can minimise conflict for everyone involved. Review their file carefully, especially their background information and medical history.

  • Does the person have any triggers that might cause them undue stress?
  • Do they have certain habits or routines that they don’t like having disrupted?
  • Do they have personality traits (e.g. stubbornness) that need to be considered?

If conflict does arise, don’t take it personally. It is usually about something specific, a particular idea, decision or action. It could be that the person is just having a bad day. Regardless of the reason, the key to resolving conflict requires empathy, understanding, patience, reflection, communication, respect, and the acknowledgment of others. In situations where you are unable to come to an agreement, speak with your supervisor about what you should do next.
