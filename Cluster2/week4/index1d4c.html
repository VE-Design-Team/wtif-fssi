Community participation

Community participation is our ability to interact and take part in society. This can range from attending school, being a customer at local businesses, volunteering for various programs and events, doing paid work, or socialising. There are several personal, societal and systemic factors that affect how an individual can participate in the community.

Select the images to learn more about the three factors affecting community participation or use the arrows to move through the images.

Personal

Personal factors include the individual’s health, mobility, socio-economic status and support networks. The better these are, the more advantageous the individual will be within the community and will lead to having a better quality of life. A person’s life experiences can also have a huge impact in terms of building their self-confidence and openness to try new things and engage with different people.

Societal

Societal factors refer to accessibility, attitudes and actions. Is the individual able to access all parts of the community like everyone else? This includes buildings, transportation, information, products and services. Other people’s attitudes and actions can encourage or discourage an individual’s participation in the community. If extremely negative, it can lead to social exclusion and isolation.

Systemic

Systemic factors are especially relevant to people with disabilities. These factors include the accessibility and quality of available support services and the type of opportunities they have for education, employment and participation in the community.
