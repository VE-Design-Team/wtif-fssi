Ways to build and strengthen networks

A network is a group of people who you care about and turn to for support and encouragement. Family, friends, work colleagues, team mates and neighbours are examples of the type of people that make up a network. For people with disabilities, networks can be challenging to build but are especially important for their well-being.

They may rely on others to assist them with their day-to-day lives, and they are at a higher risk of social isolation and exclusion. Having a good network will not only make it safer for a person with a disability, but it can help build confidence, purpose and drive. Interacting with people outside of their immediate support circle can introduce them to new ideas and interests worth pursuing.

Things to consider:

  • What kind of relationships do they want to build? Is it with people they share particular interests or hobbies with?
  • What support do they need to build these networks?
  • Where can we find the type of people they want to build a network with? At a cultural centre, sports club, community event or through social media?
  • Are there opportunities for regular meetings to strengthen the relationships with people in their network? For example, being part of a walking club that does trips every weekend, or getting to know the staff at their favourite cafe and being able to chat with them every morning while having coffee.

Online resources to find disability groups and peer support networks include the Better Health Network and Peer Connect.
