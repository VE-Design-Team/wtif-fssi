Health

Select each of the cards to flip and reveal more about the different factors leading to poorer health outcomes for people with profound or severe disabilities.

They are more likely to take up smoking at an earlier age and be daily smokers.

This puts them at a higher risk for developing lung cancer, asthma, heart disease, and stroke.

They are more likely to be overweight or obese.

This puts them at a higher risk for developing heart disease, diabetes, some cancers, and osteoarthritis.

Those with intellectual disabilities are less aware and less likely to follow healthy habits and practices.

This means it may be more difficult to help them establish healthier eating patterns or do enough daily physical activity.

There may be physical barriers that limit their access to health services.

For example, they may live in a remote area with few transportation options.

There may be organisational barriers that limit their access to health services.

For example, healthcare staff may be discriminatory towards a person with a disability and not provide them with sufficient information.
