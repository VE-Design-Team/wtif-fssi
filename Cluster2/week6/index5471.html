Power and importance

Power is the ability to do something in a certain way. Importance comes from feeling valued. It is the de-valuing of people with disabilities that makes them powerless and more vulnerable to abuse and violence.

The philosophy and values in the Australian disability sector are based on the person-centred model and their importance as an individual. This model emphasises empowering and respecting people with disabilities, giving them the abilities to make decisions that affect their quality of life.

In order to give people with disabilities power you need to encourage them to be more assertive and to know their rights. It also requires changing the way society views and treats them by challenging discrimination and promoting equality.
