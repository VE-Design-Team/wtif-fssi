Resources to compliment strengths

There are a wide range of resources available to people with disabilities through the NDIS to help them achieve greater autonomy, independence, and to improve their quality of life.

When researching and deciding on which resources to access, it is important to consider what equipment and which services best complements the individual’s strength, skills, and competencies.

 

Select each of the headings to learn more about the resources available to people with disabilities through the NDIS. When you have finished, select again to close the section.

Accommodation

General accommodation, tenancy advice, and specialist disability accommodation.

The aim is to enable people with disabilities to live as independently as possible. This includes:

  • teaching skills for managing a household (e.g. cleaning and maintenance) and finances
  • personal care support (e.g. helping with showering or dressing)
  • home assistance (e.g. cleaning and laundry aid)
  • home modifications (e.g. installing grab rails or a stair climber)
  • help with tenancy issues and legal advice
  • funding for necessary specialised housing.

Employment

Accessing and maintaining employment. This includes:

  • finding and understanding available study options
  • aiding the transition between school and further education
  • assistance in finding employment
  • advice and services to help people maintain employment
  • skills development (e.g. for language and communication, social and work interactions, personal care).

Plan management

Coordinating and managing service providers and funding plans. This includes:

  • ensuring people with disabilities have choice and control over their selected service providers, including when and how supports are delivered
  • helping service users understand the different plans available to them (e.g. self-funding, using a registered plan management provider, or having the NDIA manage their plan)
  • ensuring people with disabilities and their families understand how their NDIS plan can support their goals and manage their resources
  • identifying appropriate service providers and negotiating service agreements.

Mobility

Mobility assistance. This includes:

  • transport funding
  • necessary personal mobility equipment (e.g. mobility scooters, wheelchairs)
  • specialised driver training to help people with disabilities obtain a driver’s licence 
  • accessing and installing necessary vehicle modifications (e.g. steering aids, extra mirrors, vehicle hoists and platforms, special brake and accelerator systems).

Social support

Social support, community participation, and behavioural support. This includes:

  • interpretation and translation services
  • group and centre activities for building social connections, encouraging physical activity and exercise, and managing health (general or specific issues)
  • skill development and promoting independence through recreation and leisure activities (e.g. camps, classes, sports, coaching)
  • intensive behaviour intervention for problem behaviours
  • positive behaviour development and management
  • individualised social skills development.

Specialised services

Specialised services and equipment. This includes:

  • community nursing care
  • rehabilitation and allied health services (e.g. physiotherapy, occupational therapy, speech pathology, dietetics and nutrition, podiatry)
  • flexible at home or centre-based respite care
  • disability-specific equipment and services (e.g. guide dogs for people with low vision/blind, cochlear implants for people who are deaf, custom prosthetics)
  • communication and information equipment
  • assistive technology (e.g. environmental control software to operate domestic devices from a distance, programmable memory aids).
