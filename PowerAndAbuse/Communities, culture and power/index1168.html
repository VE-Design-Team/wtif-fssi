The past

In the past, the aged care and disability support industry have made a number of mistakes and it is important to acknowledge them so that we don’t repeat them.

Select the images to learn more about the way the aged care and disability support industry treated people in the past or use the arrows to move through the images.

For many decades, people with disability were placed in institutions, often from a very young age. These institutions treated them very badly, often using drugs and physical restraints to force those living there to be compliant.

These institutions may have been characterised by high rates of physical and emotional abuse by staff. Parents and families went along because they saw no alternative.

Staff who witnessed abuse by other staff turned a blind eye to what was going on. These institutions operated for many decades and were widely portrayed to be a normal and natural way for society to ‘deal’ with people with disability, especially those with severe physical, emotional and mental impairments.

Often, those who saw the injustice of what was happening would stand idly by because they were “forced by circumstances to make the best of a bad job”.

In this particular example, power has been exercised not just in carrying out the abuse, but also by being able to hide it.

When people think they cannot speak up and complain or when they feel fear about complaining because of what will happen to them, they cannot be free. Abuse occurs in places that have been designed to hide them.
