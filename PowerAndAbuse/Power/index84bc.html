Power and abuse in society

This course will help you understand more about power and abuse; not just obvious things between two individuals but incidents of power and abuse ranging from subtle moments to larger issues relating to groups of people in society.

Select each of the check boxes to reveal ideas about power and abuse that should be considered as you go through the course.
  • Power is exercised not just through individual relationships, but also through group and structural settings.
  • Simple things like a door being closed or words used can have a profound impact on the likelihood of something going wrong.
  • Extreme cases involving power and abuse go beyond the individuals directly involved.
  • Clients, their families, workers, managers and owners all operate within a society that privileges some to do whatever they want most of the time, even over others. This may be reinforced by enlisting the support of others.
  • Often the exercise of power (and abuse) is not just about an individual behaving badly. They are drawing upon broader societal structures to get their way.
