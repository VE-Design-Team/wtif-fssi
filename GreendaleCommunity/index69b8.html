The wider healthcare and community sector

As a support worker in aged care and disability, you are part of the broader health and community services sector. There will be other service providers, called allied health services, that will come in to provide support to the people you are working with. You will work closely with these allied services. Support may be provided through this range of services and is coordinated through a case manager. However, once a service user is in permanent residential care, then the residential facility manager will take over the coordination of support and care. A service user’s care plan, where all their care needs are clearly detailed, is reviewed monthly unless required sooner. You will be expected to work together with allied health professionals, following this combined plan for the service user’s total care. This multi-disciplinary team will lead a holistic approach to the individual’s care. Therefore, it is important that you understand some of the key areas and their roles. You will find information about the services required to support each service user in their individual care plan. Let’s look at the different parts of the health and community services sector to find out more about their role.

Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander health

Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people have a higher risk of disease and lower life expectancy compared to other Australians. People working in this area offer culturally appropriate health and community services to try to close this gap. Aboriginal Health Workers provide:

  • access to health services
  • liaison between services
  • health education and promotion
  • preventative services
  • advocacy
  • in-hospital support.

Community development

Community development is more a process or method than a sector. It aims to empower communities to become stronger and more resilient. The members of the community are the experts and workers who use community development methods to support communities to:

  • identify assets, needs, opportunities, rights and responsibilities
  • set goals and act to achieve them
  • develop activities and services to address inequality and raise public awareness
  • act as a link between the community, volunteers and local authorities.

Social work

Social work describes a range of activities, usually performed by community based, non-profit or not-for-profit associations or volunteers. Social work can include:

  • providing information and advice
  • counselling
  • advocacy
  • providing support.

Disability services

The disability services sector provides a range of services to support people with disability. Employees and volunteers in this sector work to support and empower people with disability to live fulfilling lives. Some of the services to support this include:

  • in-home support
  • accommodation
  • community-based activities
  • day programs
  • supported employment
  • respite services
  • recreation programs.

Family and domestic violence

This sector comprises a mixture of volunteer, community, not-for-profit and non-profit associations who support those experiencing family or domestic violence. The types of services provided include:

  • counselling and referrals
  • assistance accessing payments
  • legal advice and support
  • emergency accommodation
  • advocacy
  • case management.

Mental health

A variety of mental health services are involved in assessing and treating people with mental illness. The organisations offering these services include community organisations, volunteers and medical professionals. Specialised mental health services are available for families, Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people, new mothers with post-natal depression, war veterans and people with brain injuries or conditions, eating disorders or personality disorders. These services often require a referral from a GP.

Occupational therapy

Occupational therapists work to enable people to participate in day-to-day activities. They do this by working with individuals to help re-design tasks or the environment, providing adaptive equipment and monitoring progress.
