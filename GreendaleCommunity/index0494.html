OHS/WHS: your responsibility

Occupational health and safety (OHS) is a set of guidelines and safe work practice procedures. In Victoria, we follow OHS legislation, whereas most other states and territories call it workplace health and safety (WHS). You may see both WHS and OHS used in organisations. No matter the term used, the meaning and impact for you is the same: staying safe in the workplace. OHS ensures that all staff, residents and visitors remain safe, and staff are able to assess any risks that could occur. The objective of OHS law is to prevent death, injury or illness in all workplaces through workplace activities. You must maintain the safety of the service users you support, yourself and your co-workers. Everyone, including you, has a responsibility for ensuring workplaces are safe. You will learn more about how you do this throughout your training.

The following table describes the responsibilities of both workers and employers and organisations in relation to OHS.

Employers must: All workers must:
  • Maintain a safe working environment
  • Monitor work and health conditions at work
  • Provide equipment that is safe to use
  • Maintain equipment
  • Provide training to workers
  • Provide supervision to workers
  • Keep information and records about health and safety
  • Consult with employees.
  • Cooperate with safety requirements
  • Follow safety procedures
  • Report injuries
  • Work in a way that does not harm others
  • Make a claim if injured at work.
