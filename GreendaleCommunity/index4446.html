Keeping up to date

There are regular updates and changes to legislation or workplace policies and procedures. There are many reasons for this, including:

  • new evidence-based research
  • changes in government funding
  • new ways to ensure that everyone in the sector is applying best practice.

It is your responsibility to keep up to date with relevant changes within your sector.

Alert

The most up to date sources of information on legislation are the official government legislation websites:

  • Federal Register of Legislation
  • Victorian (state) Legislation.

Understanding how to apply changes in legislation, policies and procedures is the most important part for you. What do the changes mean for you and your role? Your organisation will often tell you what you must do. They may send out memos, provide written documentation or require you to attend compulsory ‘in service’ training events. You should also actively source relevant information to your role and keep learning. Look out for networking events in aged care (conferences, informal workplace conversations) and check the media, including aged care sector newsletters, either online or printed.

Note:

Always check the date on written material (either online or printed). Sometimes you can find an article written long before the law changed. It does not mean it is not relevant to you; you will just have to be more careful reading and interpreting it.

Self check

  1. Locate one online media source that writes about the aged care or disability sectors.
  2. Discuss a recent topic on the site with your teacher.
