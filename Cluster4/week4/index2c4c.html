Respiratory system

The respiratory system is designed to take in oxygen (O2) from the air, and expel carbon dioxide (CO2) from the body as waste. It also allows us to speak and communicate.

There are three main parts of the respiratory system.

Select each of the headings below to learn more about three main parts to the respiratory system.
  • Airway
  • Lungs
  • Respiratory muscles

The airway carries air from outside the body and transports it to the lungs.

The lungs are where oxygen from the air is absorbed into the blood and carbon dioxide (CO2) is removed as waste.

Respiratory muscles expand and collapse the chest cavity causing air to be inhaled and exhaled.

Several organs in the respiratory system are lined with minute hairs and mucus that trap foreign particles like dust, pollen, and contaminants and prevent them from reaching the lungs. Contaminated mucus is moved back through the respiratory system to the pharynx, where it can be either swallowed or coughed up and spat or sneezed out.
