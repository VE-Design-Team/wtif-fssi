Common issues with the musculoskeletal system

The following are some common injuries, diseases and other issues that affect the musculoskeletal system.

Select each of the headings below to learn more about some of the common issues with the musculoskeletal system.

Fractured and broken bones

Fractured and broken bones occur when excessive pressure is placed on a bone, causing it to split or break. It can happen as a result of trauma (car accidents, falls, impact), overuse (repetitive motion during work or sport), or disease that weakens the bone (as in osteoporosis). Bones can also be weakened by a poor diet and a lack of calcium or vitamin D. Broken bones that protrude from the skin or involve an open wound are called ‘open fractures’. These injuries are particularly serious as they can allow infection to enter the wound and potentially reach the bone.

Strained and torn muscles

Damage to muscle fibres can occur when a muscle is fatigued or placed under too much pressure. Muscle damage can occur during exercise, through overuse, or by fatiguing the muscle through holding sustained postures. Symptoms of a pulled, strained, or torn muscle include tightness, bruising, weakness, and an inability to stretch.

Arthritis

Arthritis is the name for a set of diseases affecting the joints of the body, causing pain, inflammation, and a reduced range of motion. The three most common forms of arthritis in Australia are:

  • osteoarthritis, a degenerative condition caused by wear and tear
  • rheumatoid arthritis, an autoimmune condition which sends antibodies to the lining of joints. The antibodies attack the tissue surrounding the joint
  • gout, a build-up of waste material (called uric acid) that crystallises in the joints.

Osteoporosis

Osteoporosis is the deterioration of bone tissue, causing bones to lose strength and break more easily. It mainly affects older women following the hormonal changes of menopause, but can be exacerbated by smoking, a sedentary lifestyle, and a lack of minerals essential to bone health such as calcium and vitamin D.

Cancer

Primary bone cancer is a rare form of cancer that begins in the bones. Secondary bone cancer is cancer that begins elsewhere in the body and spreads to the bones. Primary bone cancers most often affect the long bones of the arms and legs. However, there are more than 30 types of primary bone cancer, each affecting different bones, cartilage, ligaments, tendons and muscles.

Muscular dystrophy

Muscular dystrophy is a group of muscle-wasting disorders characterised by a progressive loss of muscle mass and strength. The condition is genetic and can lead to an inability to walk, shortening of muscles and tendons, breathing problems, heart problems, and curvature of the spine. There is no cure for muscular dystrophy, but physical therapy and medication can slow the progress of the disease.
