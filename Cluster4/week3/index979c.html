Elder abuse

Elder abuse is behaviour that causes physical, psychological or financial harm to an older person. It occurs when there is a relationship of trust between the older person and their abuser. The following table shows some of the signs of elder abuse.

Physical abuse Financial abuse Psychological or social abuse Neglect Social abuse Sexual abuse
Unexplained injuries Missing belongings or assets Resignation, withdrawal or shame Inadequate or dirty clothing Sadness or grief Internal injuries
Unexplained pain or restrictions in movement Unfamiliar signatures on cheques Depression or fearfulness Malnourishment or unexplained weight loss Withdrawal Unexplained STD
Cringing or acting fearfully Decline in spending habits Feelings of helplessness Poor hygiene, wounds or infections Low self-esteem Trauma around the genitals
Unexplained accidents Transfer of assets Insomnia Over or under medication Shame Torn or bloody bedding or underclothing
Conflicting accounts for injuries Stockpiling of unpaid bills Unusual passivity or anger Hypothermia or overheating Worry or anxiety about visiting someone Changes in sleep patterns. Shame, anxiety or withdrawal
