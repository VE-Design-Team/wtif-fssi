Immune system

The immune system is the bodyâ€™s natural defence system, protecting it from foreign bodies that might enter and cause disease or damage. The immune system works closely with many other body systems to identify threats, fight potential invaders, and eliminate them from the body. In previous sections we looked at how other organs and biological systems work with the immune system to protect the body.

Flip the cards to learn more about some of the ways that the immune system works with other body systems.

The skin acts as a barrier to prevent the entry of foreign bodies.

Integumentary

The ears produce wax that traps dust and germs before they can reach the brain.

Nervous

The stomach produces acid that kills harmful bacteria ingested in food.

Digestive

The liver filters toxins and sends them through the body to be eliminated.

Digestive

The kidneys remove waste and toxins in the blood before they can damage cells and tissues.

Urinary

Mucus in the airways and lungs traps particles and germs inhaled in the air.

Respiratory

Blood vessels prevent cancer cells, bacteria and other damaging particles from entering the blood stream.

Circulatory

Lymph vessel walls allow cancer cells, bacteria and other germs to escape the body tissues and enter the lymph fluid where they are filtered and eliminated.
