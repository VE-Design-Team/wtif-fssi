Proprioceptive senses

In addition to the five main senses, there are two extra senses that help us register the position and movement of our body in space. Called ‘proprioceptive senses’, these include vestibular sense and kinaesthesia.

Vestibular Sense

Vestibular sense gives us information about the position of our body in space. Organs for vestibular sense are in the inner ear. When our head moves, these organs sense changes in gravity and movement related to the position of our head. This helps us to balance and stay upright.

Kinaesthesia

Kinaesthesia gives us information about the position of our limbs and other body parts relative to one another. For instance, it lets us judge where our arms are in relation to our legs, or where our hand is in relation to our mouth. This allows us to perform complex movements like walking, eating or washing our hair. The receptors for kinaesthesia are in the joints, tendons and muscles.
