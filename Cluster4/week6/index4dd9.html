Common issues with the urinary system

Select each of the headings below to learn more about some of the common issues with the urinary system.

Kidney Disease

Chronic kidney disease leads to waste and toxins accumulating in the blood, causing a range of illnesses over time. The most common causes of kidney disease are diabetes and high blood pressure. If the kidneys fail completely, dialysis treatments will be necessary to keep the person alive.

Urinary Incontinence

Urinary incontinence is a common condition that causes a lack of control over urination. It can be a symptom of a wider problem (like a UTI) or be a temporary issue due to pregnancy. There are four main types of urinary incontinence:

  • stress incontinence (leaking small amounts of urine while sneezing, laughing, playing sport etc.)
  • urge incontinence (a strong, sudden, uncontrollable need to urinate)
  • chronic retention (frequent leakage of urine due to the bladder failing to empty properly during toilet visits)
  • functional incontinence (normal bladder function hindered by an inability to get to a toilet).

Urinary Tract Infection (UTI)

Urinary tract infections (UTIs) can affect the bladder, urethra, and kidneys. They occur when bacteria from outside the body enter the urinary tract, often following sexual activity or with the use of internal products such as diaphragms and spermicide. Due to women’s shorter urethra, bacteria have less distance to travel to reach the bladder, making UTIs four times more common in women than in men. The most common type of UTI is cystitis, affecting over a third of women during their lifetime.

Symptoms of a UTI include a persistent need to urinate, a burning sensation, cloudy, red-coloured, or smelly urine, fever, and pain around the pelvis and pubic bone.

Bladder Cancer

Bladder cancer affects the lining of the bladder, and occurs more often in older men. A common symptom of bladder cancer is blood in the urine, making it appear dark yellow, brown, or bright red. The greatest risk factor for bladder cancer is cigarette smoking.
