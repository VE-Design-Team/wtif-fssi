Ambient Assistive Technology (AAT)

SHT can be used to extend the time in which people with disability and older people can live independently. This is known as Ambient Assistive Living (AAL).

Select each of the headings to learn about some of the ways in which AAT can assist people. When you have finished, select again to close the section.

Remote control

People can turn things on or off remotely without having to move around.

For example, when the doorbell is pressed, the house lights flash and the resident’s voice command can automatically open the door for the visitor.

Voice-activated internet

People can retrieve information from the internet through voice activation systems like Alexa or Google Home.

Cognitive assistance

Cognitive assistance includes programmed schedules, reminders and step-by-step guidance for performing tasks. This can even be used to encourage behavioural changes. For example, the system can provide step-by-step instructions on how to brush one’s teeth:

Getting the toothbrush, putting toothpaste on it, brushing in circular motions over one’s teeth, and rinsing and cleaning the toothbrush.

As the user learns and remembers how to brush his or her teeth, fewer instructions are given.

Monitoring and sensoring

These functions can detect changes in temperature, motion, sound and vision. This can assist with safety, security and even cognitive function.

It can detect when a person has fallen or when a person has not followed their normal pattern of movement (e.g. not gotten out of bed or gone to the toilet at the usual time) and alert their family or support worker.

Sensors can even be connected to personal items. For example, if a person leaves the house without their keys or wallet, the system will alert them.
