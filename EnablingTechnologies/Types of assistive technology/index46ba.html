Skills outcomes for people using technology

Engagement with digital technology can significantly impact the skills, leisure and relationships of people with disability and older people.

Select each of the headings to learn how the use of digital technology can impact people with disability and older people.

  • Aged care
  • Challenges

Computers and mobile devices play a huge role in supporting education and employment for people with disability. Studies have shown that people with disability gain significant skills, including problem-solving abilities, improved accuracy in tasks, higher work outputs and increased independence in the workplace by using mobile devices such as iPads.

IT devices have also been shown to help develop independence in home-based routines such as meal preparation and self-care. In school environments, tablet devices have led to better educational outcomes for people with disability, including learning cause and effect and improved social participation and motor skills.  

For older people in aged care settings or those who want to live independently in their own homes, being able to prepare and cook meals, and manage timetables is vital. Digital technology has been found to help older people maintain visual and motor skills and support activity engagement.

Larger keyboards on computers, touch screens, speech and voice recognition, screen enlargers and magnifiers, and software programs that use speech synthesizers for auditory feedback can encourage older people to read, write and communicate. Mobile devices are especially useful for individuals confined to sitting in one spot or in bed.  

For all the positive skills outcomes associated with digital technology, challenges remain. Apps for people with disability should include different ways of aiding language processing by including pictographs, text-to-speech or language simplification options. They need to be linked to computer networks and councils should integrate public and mobile access.

We have to embrace the ‘social view’ and ensure that the design of our infrastructure accommodates a diverse range of needs so that the future will be all-inclusive.
