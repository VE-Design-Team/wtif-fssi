Support workers, users and organisations

The use of computers and devices in the support services sectors affects workers, clients and organisations in different ways and their relationships with each other.

Select each of the tabs to learn more about how technology is changing the way support workers, users and service providers work and interact.
Support worker

Support workers could have:

  • remote access rostering, documentation and record-keeping
  • online training and access to policies and procedures
  • ability to stay up-to-date and maintain computer and IT skills.
Service user

Support users could have:

  • increased stimulation, connection, belonging and overall well-being
  • increased skills and development
  • increased independence
  • improved relationships and behaviour.
Support service provider

Support service providers could have:

  • improved efficiency
  • financial savings
  • a mobile workforce
  • stronger partnerships and networking
  • faster and more informed decision making
  • improved client outcomes.
