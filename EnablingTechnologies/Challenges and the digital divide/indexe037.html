Adoption and uptake

Part of your role as a support worker in the aged care and disability support industry is to assist people with using technology. It may be as simple as always making sure their walking frame is within easy reach or sitting down with them and assisting them to use an iPad. In many ways, ensuring the adoption and uptake of AT overlaps with many other parts of your job.

What AT needs to be 

Person-centred

The engagement of the support worker with the client must always be person-centred and this is no less true in relation to AT.

Time and work

It is important to understand that clients may have a view of AT that puts them off adopting it.Â Whatever their reason, it may take time and work to convince the person that AT is a useful approach.

Knowing the person

Knowing the person you are supporting is a vital part of providing person centred care. This includes knowing how to introduce and integrate new technology into a support user’s life so that it better reflects their wishes.

Client participation

By making sure the clients participate and co-design the solutions to their AT needs, you will maximise uptake and continued use. For example, demonstrating what things can do may encourage them to give it a go.

Involving others

Family carers and other staff may also need to learn about the technology and how to use it. This will provide a constant level of support across settings.

What AT needs to be

AT needs to:

  1. be valuable by addressing practical, emotional and relational challenges
  2. fit well into, or be a better solution for, habitual practice and established strategies
  3. generate positive emotions and become a reliable and trustworthy tool
  4. be user-friendly, adaptable and manageable
  5. be of interest and engage the family carer.
