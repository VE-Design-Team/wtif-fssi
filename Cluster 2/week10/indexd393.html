Keeping a record

Regardless of whether abuse is suspected, witnessed, or disclosed, the victimâ€™s safety and wellbeing should be safeguarded right away. After reporting an allegation of abuse to management, keep a record of what happened by writing it down. What did the victim tell you? Who was involved? What did you see or hear?

Give your notes to your supervisor or the person responsible for handling the abuse allegations, unless there is a potential conflict or if they are involved in the allegation. If you have contact with the police or other investigators, tell them you kept notes and provide them with a copy if requested.

Make sure you keep a copy of your notes in a secure location. Do not share any information about the allegation with other staff unless a relevant manager has given you permission.

Select each of the check boxes to learn more about what information to write down following a report of abuse.
  • A description of what the victim told you, in their exact words if possible.
  • If you or someone else witnessed the abuse, what was seen and heard? Make sure you stick to the facts. Do not include your opinions or interpretations. Do not interview the person accused of the abuse.
  • Details of any witnesses, including their names, addresses, and contact details.
  • A description of what you saw and the actions you took. Did you intervene, seek help from another support worker, or immediately call the police or your supervisor?
  • Sign and date this document.
