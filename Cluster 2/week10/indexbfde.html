Emotional abuse

Select each of the headings to learn about some of the signs of mental or emotional distress. When you have finished, select again to close the section.

Depression

Depression is a persistent feeling of sadness that does not go away. The person may appear tearful, low energy, or apathetic, by displaying a lack of interest in things or activities that used to bring them joy.

Behaviours of passivity, being disengaged with life, or resignation, thinking that they cannot change the outcome of things, are also indicators of depression. Depression can also lead to disrupted sleep patterns or changes in appetite.

Anxiety

Other behaviours associated with anxiety are confusion, not being able to think clearly, or agitation, being restless or feeling on edge.

Withdrawal

Withdrawal is when a person stops taking part in activities or interacting with others. It is an unexplained or self-imposed isolation.

Paranoia or Fear

Paranoia is a mistrust of other people without reason. This is often accompanied by an irrational fear and suspicion of others, believing that everyone is out to cause them harm. A lack of eye contact or nervousness around certain people can be a sign of mental and emotional stress.
