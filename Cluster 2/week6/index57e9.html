Financial

In Australia, 45 percent of people with disability live in poverty of near poverty. This means they tend to have lower incomes, are less likely to be employed, and are more likely to be dependent on income support.

People with a disability in Australia have a relative income of 70 percent compared to people without a disability. This is the lowest relative income of all 27 countries in the Organisation for Economic Cooperation and Development (OECD). In other countries, such as Mexico, there is no income difference between people with and without disabilities.

Employment

People with disabilities are more likely to be unemployed, especially women with disabilities. They tend to get paid less and work predominately in part-time positions. Compare this to people without disabilities who are paid more for the same roles with greater full-time employment opportunities.

Employment is not only a source of income but also a place for people with disabilities to develop their social skills and networks, build their confidence and identity, and have greater autonomy and independence.

Education

People with disabilities, not just those with intellectual disabilities, generally have lower levels of education.

They often go to special schools despite evidence that children with intellectual disabilities fare better socially and academically when attending mainstream schools.

Even with the same education level, people with disabilities have lower incomes because they have a harder time finding employment and are usually paid less than people without disabilities.

Housing

People with disabilities are often living in housing situations that are inadequate, leading to poorer health and social outcomes.

Ninety-five percent of people with disabilities live in households rather than residential facilities. Even though it might be more beneficial for some to live in these facilities, the costs can make it impossible.

This becomes even more problematic for individuals who rely on aging family members for care. The decision of where the person with a disability can live after their family members are too old to provide support can be a hard financial one.

The rate of people with disabilities living in public housing is twice that of the rest of the population. A reason for this is because they are at a disadvantage in the housing market. Having lower incomes and additional costs for disability support make it difficult to achieve and maintain home ownership
