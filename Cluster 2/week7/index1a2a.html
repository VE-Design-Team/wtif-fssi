Making a referral

Helen is a support worker for Ashraf, who has low vision. Ashraf has recently moved from Perth to Melbourne to live with his brother and family.

Ashraf tells Helen that he has two young nieces that enjoy playing outdoors but he has been having trouble keeping up with them. His previous doctor in Perth had told him that he was borderline overweight, but he had trouble losing weight on his own. Being able to walk, hike, and play outdoors with his nieces without having to frequently stop for breaks to catch his breath and rest has become a big motivator for Ashraf to lose weight and be healthier.

Select each of the headings to learn more about how Helen can support Ashraf with his weight loss goal through referrals. When you have finished, select again to close the section.

Identifying needs

Ashraf’s goal is to lose weight and be healthier. The best way to achieve this goal is to adjust his diet and increase his physical activity.

Helen is not an expert in diet, nutrition, or physical activity so she will have to refer Ashraf to people or organisations that are. Before they begin looking at different referral options, she asks Ashraf if there are any conditions or personal preferences that need to be considered that would direct their search.

Ashraf states that, because of his Muslim faith, any changes to his diet must adhere to Islamic dietary laws.

In his previous attempt to be more physically active, Ashraf went to a gym with a personal trainer, however, he did not have a positive experience. The exercises felt like work, the sessions were expensive, and he did not enjoy the gym environment. He would prefer being outdoors in a more social environment as he thinks it would make exercise more interesting and enjoyable.

Making a referral

From her discussions with Ashraf, Helen suggests a dietician who has experience working with service users of Muslim faith and devising meal plans to suit their cultural backgrounds and beliefs.

In terms of physical activity, Helen thinks speaking with an occupational therapist and community development worker would help direct Ashraf to outdoor group activities or team sports that would fit his abilities and goals. Ashraf is happy with Helen’s recommendations.

Helen speaks with her supervisor about the referrals she would like. Her supervisor agrees with the steps she would like to take. Helen completes the appropriate paperwork to make the referrals for Ashraf.

Monitoring and support

After Ashraf has consulted with the dietician, occupational therapist, and community development worker Helen referred him to, they sit down to review his experience with the specialists and their proposed new diet and physical activity plans.

Ashraf was very positive about his meetings with the specialists and would like to proceed with their recommendations. Helen includes Ashraf’s brother and family in their next discussions, so they can integrate Ashraf’s dietary plans into their regular family meals.

From meeting with the occupational therapist and community development worker, Ashraf has discovered a local blind cricket team. Since he and his brother are big cricket fans, Ashraf is excited at the prospect of being able to play. The specialists have put him in touch with people at the Victorian Blind Cricket Association to get started.

Helen helps Ashraf set-up and organise his new diet and begin blind cricket (e.g. getting to trainings and games, access to equipment, socialising with teammates and coaching staff). Over the next few months, Helen regularly follows-up with Ashraf and his family to see how he is progressing.

Feedback

After Ashraf first met with the dietician, occupational therapist, and community development worker, Helen asked Ashraf how the consultations went. Did he feel comfortable talking to them? Were they respectful and listen to what he had to say? Were they knowledgeable and take the time to explain things that were unclear and answer his questions? Was he happy with their recommendations?

Over the next few months, as Ashraf has follow-up consultations with the specialists and continues with the new diet and physical activities, Helen checks that Ashraf’s experiences are still positive.

Helen reports to her supervisor about how Ashraf’s referrals have gone. As Ashraf has been feeling very encouraged and is seeing an improvement in his weight and health, Helen and her supervisor feel confident about working with and referring future patients to these specialists.
