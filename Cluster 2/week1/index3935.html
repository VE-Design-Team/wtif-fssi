Disability and cultural differences

Disability is a socially constructed concept. This means that the way people with disabilities are treated depends on how society has defined and understood disability.

The way people view disability is influenced by their cultural and religious backgrounds. It is important not to make assumptions and to be sensitive to the cultural beliefs and traditions of service users. Consider the following scenarios:

Question 1 of 2

Some cultures believe a disability is punishment for a person’s or family’s past actions. They see the disability to be shameful and try to hide it from others. This belief makes them reluctant to seek out support services and can lead to social isolation of the person with the disability as well as their family.
