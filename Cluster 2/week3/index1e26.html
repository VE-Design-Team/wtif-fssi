Promoting independence

According to the UN Convention on the Rights of People with Disability, independence is defined as the individual’s right to autonomy. This means they should play an active role in making decisions about how they live and be socially inclusive, where they have the same opportunities and resources available to them as everyone else. Promoting independence in people with disabilities involves building confidence. They need to be encouraged to make decisions in as many parts of their lives as possible.

They should feel safe making mistakes and asking for help. Equally important is making reliable resources and assistive aids that enable them to perform tasks on their own easily accessible. While the term ‘independence’ may emphasise doing things on your own, for people with disabilities it is mainly about being a valued member of the community. There are three themes that impact a service user’s opportunities for independence: identity development, resources and support, and attitudes.

Select each of the cards to flip and reveal more about the three themes that impact independence for people with disabilities.

Identity development is learning who we are through the experiences we have. It is tied to the opportunities we have to make meaningful decisions.

Support and resources refer to our networks and access to information and services.

Attitudes are how other people view you as an individual.
