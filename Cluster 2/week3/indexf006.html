Resources and supports

The second theme impacting independence is resources and supports. The biggest contributor to a person with disability’s independence is the resources and support they receive from their family. How nurturing and capable a family is of advocating for the person depends on many factors, like what socio-economic status they’re in, the networks they have, and their prior experience with disability. There is a strong correlation between a family’s skills, time and finances, and the quality of support and degree of independence the person with a disability experiences.

Consider how the following example support and resource factors might affect the independence of a person with a disability.

Question 2 of 5

A stay-at-home parent looking after their child with a disability as well as an elderly grandparent. Would this give the child with disability more or less independence?



Incorrect

Having to look after a child with a disability, an elderly grandparent, and other household and family responsibilities means the stay-at-home parent would have less time to spend with their child and to look into what resources and supports would be most beneficial.

Correct

Question 3 of 5

Having highly educated family members. Would this give a person with disability more or less independence?



Correct

Highly educated family members might have more connections or know how to find information about available and accessible resources and supports for a person with disability.

Incorrect

Question 4 of 5

Having family members working multiple low-paying jobs to cover costs for disability support services. Would this give a person with disability more or less independence?



Incorrect

While the family members may be working hard to give the person with disability access to support services, they themselves would have less time to spend with the person with disability and to ensure that they were getting access to the appropriate resources and support.

Correct
