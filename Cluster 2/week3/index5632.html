Role of support workers

The role of a support worker is to help people with disability live an ordinary life. Service users have the same goals and aspirations as people without disabilities, such as building and maintaining meaningful relationships, being able to contribute to the community, and enjoying a sense of belonging.

Support workers provide formal guidance, working with the service user and their families and friends to find opportunities for them to learn, work and engage with society. There might be risks involved for a service user to try something new, but you can minimise these risks with careful planning and putting in safeguards.

Taking steps to actively reduce barriers

Addressing attitudinal, environmental and institutional barriers. For example, contacting building management about accessibility issues that are preventing the service user from entering the building or retrieving information.

Moving the service user towards greater independence in their daily life

Encourage the service user to try more flexible models of service provisions that give them greater choice and involvement.

Recognising the importance of a service user’s networks

The formal support you provide should complement the informal support of family and friends. Your work is inclusive, working with the service user and the people most important to them to provide them with the best quality of life.

Ensuring the service user is involved in making decisions that affect their life

Make sure the service user has all the information they need to fully understand the situation and that they have the chance to voice what they think.
