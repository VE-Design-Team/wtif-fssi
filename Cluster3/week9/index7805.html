Managing your own stress

Stress is a normal condition, however, it does need to be managed. While you will spend some time and energy managing the stress of others, you may need strategies to manage your own stress as well.

Express your feelings

  • Debrief at the end of each shift with a supervisor or person in charge.
  • Talk to your colleagues and employer about ongoing concerns.
  • Consider seeking support from an understanding professional or support group.
  • Talk to your family or friends and ask for their support and suggestions.

Rest and stay healthy

  • Take regular breaks.
  • Try meditation or yoga.
  • Eat healthy food and exercise regularly.
  • Create time for yourself to enjoy life and relax.

Seek professional help if needed

If work-related stress continues to be a problem despite your efforts, seek advice from a career counsellor or psychologist. Some workplaces may offer an Employer Assistance Program. Speak to your supervisor to find out what is offered at your workplace.
