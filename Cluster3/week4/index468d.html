Planning suitable activities

When planning suitable activities for people with dementia, you need to have a good knowledge of what makes them unique. Activities that suit their background and experience are likely to bring them the most joy and satisfaction. Ask yourself the following questions to help plan these:

  • What are their hobbies and interests?
  • What did they do for work?
  • What significant life events have they experienced?
  • What activities would be culturally appropriate?
  • What time of the day do they function best?
  • What actions are they able to make?

People with dementia often retain long-term memories, even as the illness progresses. Activities that include childhood memorabilia, photographs, music and tools or instruments they used or experienced can bring back memories and/or old skills. Your organisation’s physiotherapist can design an exercise program that takes the resident’s current health and abilities into account. Exercise activities might include dancing, gardening, and walking (especially good for Alzheimer’s patients who become restless and often wander). Aids and equipment can help people with dementia to participate independently in activities. Inevitably, as the disease progresses, they will require more assistance with activities or different kinds of activities may be necessary.
