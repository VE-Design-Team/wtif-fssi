Advanced personal support tasks

At times, you may identify certain personal support tasks that are not part of your role or skill set, or require you to work with equipment which you have not been trained to use. These and other advanced personal support tasks, should be performed by medical and other allied healthcare professionals.

Select the following healthcare professions to see specific advanced personal care tasks they will perform for residents.
Doctors and nurses
  • Inserting a catheter.
  • Dressing a complex wound.
  • Providing information on medical conditions to family members.
  • Giving injections.
  • Developing strategies to manage complex care needs.
Dental technicians
  • Repairing dentures.
Podiatrists
  • Performing podiatry (foot care) on diabetic residents and those who cannot perform this task themselves.
Physiotherapists
  • Assessing resident’s mobility and strength, and putting rehabilitation or maintenance plans in place to optimise mobility and minimise pain.
