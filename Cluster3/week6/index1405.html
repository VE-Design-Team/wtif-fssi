Gathering information about individual support needs

Being an informed support worker prepares you with knowledge on how to deliver quality, person-centred care. Incorporating this information into your care is vital.

Every interaction you have with residents, their family and friends, other personal care attendants and your supervisor provides you with an opportunity to gather information that will assist you in your role.

Discuss and confirm each person’s own preferences for personal support in a positive way. Remember that each individual is a person with their own likes and dislikes, joys and sorrows, experiences and personality. They used to be independent and losing independence isn’t easy.

Add any knowledge you gain through a support activity to the resident’s progress notes. Talk to your supervisor about any information gathered through informal discussions with residents and their family/friends. The supervisor will update the individual’s care plan.

Alert

When sharing information, you must respect the privacy and dignity of the resident.

Private and confidential information should be stored in accordance with your organisation’s policies and procedures.
