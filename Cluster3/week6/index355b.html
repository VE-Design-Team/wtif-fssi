Creating safe environments

Feeling safe, secure and comfortable in your living environment is important for health and well-being. Residents need to feel that their home is comfortable and that they are safe. They need freedom to move around the facility without feeling at risk of harm. Residents must feel in control of their personal security and be able to use strategies to protect themselves.

This includes having adequate shelter, living in an environment without violence, and feeling free to express themselves. In residential aged care facilities, workers use several strategies to ensure the environment is safe and comfortable.

Select each strategy to find out more about it.

Security Systems

The facility must have an adequate security system. This might include:

  • alarms and emergency systems
  • security patrols
  • adequate locks on doors
  • smoke alarms
  • electrical testing and tagging
  • adequate emergency lighting.

Comfort

The environment should be physically comfortable. This includes appropriate temperatures, lighting, smell and appearance.

It also includes being able to move around freely. In addition, the environment must be emotionally comfortable. The residents should be surrounded by things they enjoy. This may include interaction with others.

Adaptations

To ensure residents can move freely in their homes, some adaptations are required. These help to prevent injury. As people age, their sense of sight, smell and touch deteriorates. Similarly, their judgement and reaction time decreases. As a result, older people have a higher risk of injury in their personal environment. To minimise the risks, the residential aged care facility may have:

  • lever-actioned door handles rather than round knobs
  • non-slip flooring
  • temperature limits on hot water systems
  • cabinets and shelving that are easily accessible to residents
  • railings on walls and stairs larger and light switches.

Predictable Routines

Over time, people develop routines. They become accustomed to doing things in a particular way. To maintain a sense of independence, it is important for residents to maintain their routines and to do things the way they prefer, within their capabilities.

These routines should be considered when developing the resident’s care plan. Following the care plan will help you to meet the resident’s particular preferences. To maintain a safe and familiar routine, you need to deliver services at the times specified in the care plan. Before providing a service, discuss it with the resident and check that it fits with their routine.
