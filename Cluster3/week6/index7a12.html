Personal support and infection control

Personal care activities present residents and personal care workers with an increased risk of infection, especially when residents have difficulties with activities such as toileting. Your hands are a likely source of transmission, so infection control is paramount.

Make sure you locate and gain an understanding of your organisation’s infection control policy and procedure documents. If you are unsure of what to do in any situation, always seek guidance from your supervisor.

Select each of the following to learn how to practice effective infection control.

Wash your hands 

Disinfect equipment 

Manage spills 

Observe and report 

Wash your hands

  • Clean hands with soap and water.
  • Dry thoroughly.
  • Keep fingernails clean and trimmed.
  • Use hand cream to help keep skin healthy.
  • Wear gloves to reduce risk of exposure to blood or body fluids.
  • Use alcohol hand rubs when your hands are not visibly soiled.

Disinfect equipment

  • Clean with disinfectant when infection is known.
  • Clean equipment after use.
  • Clean commode chairs and shower chairs before using.
  • Use disposable equipment if available.
  • Keep the equipment for the same person (slings, princess chairs, etc).

Manage spills

  • Clean promptly using appropriate equipment and signage.
  • Ensure items are disposed of into the correct containers.
  • Dispose of waste appropriately.

Observe and report

  • Talk to your supervisor about any aspect of infection control you don’t understand.
  • Tell your supervisor if you notice anything that might increase the risk of infection.
  • Report any unsafe situations/incidents immediately.
  • Regularly audit for risk and compliance.
  • Complete incident and hazard reports as required.
