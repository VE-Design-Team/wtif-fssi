Dementia specific policies and procedures

Dementia specific policies and procedures are essential for safe and effective resident care and exist to support you in your role. Locate and familiarise yourself with your organisation’s policies and procedures for changed behaviour management and de-escalation procedures. You must alert your supervisor of any changes in a resident’s behaviour.

Your supervisor will ask you to detail:

  • the resident’s changed behaviour(s)
  • the physical and psychological risks associated with the behaviour displayed
  • possible triggers that may have led to the changed behaviour
  • strategies that will address these triggers and reduce behaviours of concern.

Your supervisor will document the information you provide. You and your supervisor will refer to these documents when communicating with the resident’s family at care plan meetings. Families will be informed of changed behaviours and any incidents that have occurred, and involved in the development of strategies to manage these new behaviours.

The following documents are examples of those used to record changed behaviour:

  • Individual Care Plan – Behaviour Management section
  • Behaviour Monitoring Log (e.g. Scotch Thistle Behaviour Monitoring log)
  • Behaviour Assessment and Management Strategies (e.g. Scotch Thistle Residential Care doc)
  • Progress Notes.

Have a go

Your organisation will have their own guidelines and documentation for reporting and following up on a resident’s changed behaviour. All documents should be stored in their relevant location. Locate these documents and discuss them with your supervisor.
