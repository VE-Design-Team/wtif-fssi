Hazards

Hazards are things, situations or behaviours that can harm you and others around you. Equipment, chemicals and poor work practices can cause hazards.

There are five classes of hazards as shown below.

Select each of the headings of the five classes of hazards.

Physical

A factor within the environment that can harm the body without necessarily touching it.

Examples:

Wet floors, vibration, noise, equipment, falling objects, travel risks, electricity.

Ergonomic

A physical factor within the environment that harms the musculoskeletal system.

Examples: 

Manual handling, work design, body positioning, lighting.

Chemical

Substances, mixtures and materials classified according to their health and physicochemical risks and dangers.

Examples:

Acids or poisons, chemicals that can lead to fire or explosion.

Biological

Organic substances that pose a threat to the health of humans and other living organisms.

Examples:

Bacteria, viruses, mould, mildew, insects, vermin or animals.

Psychosocial

Psychosocial hazards include, but are not limited to, stress, violence and other workplace stressors.

Examples:

Bullying, client aggression, alcohol or drug affected persons, fatigue.
