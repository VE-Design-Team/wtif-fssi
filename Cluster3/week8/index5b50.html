Managing behaviours of concern

You will play an important role in the detection, treatment and management of mental health concerns in residents. Each individual is unique. Ensure that you check personal care plans so that you are always up to date. Personal care plans will have instructions on dealing with known behaviours of concern for that particular individual.

Report all new behaviours of concern to your supervisor so that the person in need receives the appropriate follow up treatment and care. The following are general behaviours that form good working habits and will help you to maximise quality of life and support the specific needs of residents living with dementia and mental illness.

Select each of the options below to see how you can assist residents who have a mental illness.

Be respectful

An older person is still a person. A resident’s mental health is merely one aspect of them as a whole.

Avoid making assumptions

There is no ‘one size fits all’ rule and every resident in your care is an individual with his or her own wants, needs and preferences.

Foster social inclusion

A sense of belonging is associated with emotional happiness and mental health. Interventions that enhance a sense of belonging are useful in the prevention and treatment of depression.

Always work appropriately

Seek direction from your supervisor (RN) to ensure you support the resident appropriately. Always follow your organisation’s workplace rules and practises.

Educate yourself

Learn more about mental health by accessing helpful resources and seeking advice on how to offer support.
