Erasing identity

 

When people age, their capacity to display their identity can be erased based on people’s stereotypical assumptions about them. This is called erasure. We can forget that the older person has in fact multiple identities and that being old is only one—probably the least significant one.

We can make assumptions about what it is they like, which turn out to be fundamentally wrong. One example was in a residential aged care setting where management adopted an initiative for staff called ‘bring your best skill to work’.

This was designed to get residents enthused about a wider variety of interests. Within a month a problem arose. The staff brought their skills—knitting, electronics and so forth—but soon found they had become the pupils.

In amongst the histories of the residents, there were so many with identities and skills that were never known and never supported by the staff. The same might be said around the cultural or sexual identities of residents, that unless the person’s identity is known, they will be erased.
