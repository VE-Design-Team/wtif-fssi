Consequence control

A form of abuse that is particularly harmful and difficult to detect involves the regular exercise of coercion (as defined earlier). Here a worker, family member or friend routinely threatens to harm a client if they don’t go along with what has been asked. The client is effectively controlled by that other person because they routinely worry about the consequences.

Interestingly, this can happen by inferring harm will happen (“Wouldn’t it be terrible if…”). Sometimes words need not be exchanged; it is just known between the parties that the threat hangs as an ever-present possibility that may never actually be put into effect because the client continues to do what is wanted. Sometimes this sort of situation can arise from a misunderstanding. This is why open communication is so important.
