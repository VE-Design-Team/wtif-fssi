Definitions

Power is often confused with other related terms like authority, influence, coercion, force, control, violence, manipulation and strength. 

It is important that you understand how power and related words may impact the relationships between clients of social services and providers of those services.

It is important to know how power can be used for the benefit of clients and their families, without doing them harm.

Power

The ability of a person or group of persons to exercise their will, even against the resistance of others.

Influence

Someone who can influence the behaviour of others or affect the outcome of a situation.

Authority

Someone asking another or others to do something and they agree, even if they have doubts about what they are being asked to do, not because of fear but because of the other person’s social position (e.g. a manager or a police officer), or because of a belief that the other person knows what should be done and therefore obeying is the right thing to do.

Coercion

Getting someone to do what you want by threatening to cause them harm if they don’t or promising to do something good to them if they do (the latter is sometimes called ‘inducement’).

Force and control

Someone getting others to do what they want because they fear the consequences if they don’t comply.

Manipulation

Controlling what people think of as being ‘right’ so they agree to things that they would not normally do or agree to.

Abuse

Someone hurts someone physically or psycho-socially knowing that their behaviour or language will have this effect.
