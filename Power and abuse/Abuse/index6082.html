Power: from doing good to doing harm

Power is not only exercised to harm others, it may also be exercised to benefit them. This is sometimes referred to as the difference between ‘power over’ and ‘power to’. The exercise of power can benefit someone at the same time as it harms another. Sometimes power is exercised to protect others from more powerful people, or people who would wish to do harm. For example, when a personal care worker reports a case of abuse they have identified from a conversation with clients or observing behaviours of clients after interactions with others.
