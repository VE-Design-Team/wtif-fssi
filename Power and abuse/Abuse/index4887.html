The empowerment circle

Power can be understood on a continuum, from something involving good practice through to abuse, which may include bullying. National Disability Services (NDS) uses the following tool to show how this continuum works in relation to eight life areas.

NDS provides a suite of resources and exercises about abuse in their Zero Tolerance framework which uses the Empowerment Circle and explores human rights and abuse.

The following website provides free access to these resources: www.nds.org.au

The Centre

The Empowerment Circle is a circle diagram with a person at the centre in a dark green circle.

Three Zones

The circle is divided into three zones (represented in rings from the centre toward the outside of circle):

  • Green for good or positive practice
  • Orange for poor or neglectful practice
  • Red for abusive or criminal practice

Eight Sections

The circle is divided into eight sections, like slices of a pizza. Each section represents one area of people’s lives: physical, emotional, social, identity, material, economic, education and relationships.

Human rights arrows

Four human rights arrows, evenly spaced around the circle and pointing from the outer edges of the circle to the centre, represent the idea that the closer we are to the middle, the more we are using and enjoying our rights. The arrows are labelled ‘Freedom’, ‘Respect’, ‘Equality’ and ‘Dignity’.
