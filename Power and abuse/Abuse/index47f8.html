Scenario: mysterious bruises

As part of your role as a support worker, you do regular home visits to assist an elderly woman in a wheelchair.  She lives with her husband, who receives a carer’s allowance as his only source of income.

Work through the following scenario to check how you would respond when a resident has unexplained bruises. This is not an assessment. In each step, select the action you would take. You can try this activity as many times as you like.

You notice bruises on the woman’s arms on several visits. What do you do?

  • A. Ignore it because it is not part of your job.
  • B. Report it to the police immediately.
  • C. Ask her if she is okay.

No, this is part of your job. Unexplained bruises shouldn’t be ignored as they could not only indicate abuse but also other medical issues.

  • Go back and try again.

No, just because someone has a bruise on them doesn’t mean you should call the police straight away. She could have fallen over, or there may be some other explanation.

  • Go back and try again.

She tells you she is okay but doesn’t want to talk about it.

  • A. Take no further action.
  • B. Demand she explains how she got bruises on her arm.
  • C. Inform your supervisor.

No, she may not want to talk about it because she is afraid.

  • Go back and try again.

No, making demands of her like this is also a form of abuse.

  • Go back and try again.

Informing your supervisor is the best response. You shouldn’t ignore bruises or overreact to them either. Ask the person if they are okay and note their response.

Congratulations! You have successfully responded to a situation and followed correct procedures when you find mysterious bruises on a resident.
