Using power for good

A key part of your role is to try to make sure that the policies, procedures and practices of the organisation you work for, as well as your own behaviour, empower clients to achieve the goals they wish to achieve. Power is not always used to harm people.

It can also be used to do good. Even those with seemingly few resources can use their positions to help. A personal care worker, for example, who exposes a case of abuse is using their power to good effect. A manager who introduces a system of open disclosure is using their power wisely.
