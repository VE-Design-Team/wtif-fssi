Shaping desires and beliefs

One of the most insidious ways in which power is exercised is by the very shaping of desires and beliefs.

Select each of the check boxes to learn more about how power can be exercised through a broad array of groups, organisations and social institutions.
  • People go along with what they understand to be the dominant belief system because they can see no alternative, even though some might exist.
  • They might be resigned to their existing way of life because those with power have taught them there is no other way.
  • The powerful have control over information and cultural institutions like the mass media.
  • It has been the behaviour of many people over many decades.
  • Workers and managers actively engage in abuse and the systems in place are designed to hide it and protect the instigators.
