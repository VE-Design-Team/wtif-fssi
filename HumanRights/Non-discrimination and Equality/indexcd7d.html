Features of human rights

Human rights instruments are written documents that help create international human rights laws. There are two types of instruments:

  1. Declarations, which are not legally binding, but set agreed international standards.
  2. Conventions, which are legally binding.

These instruments define human rights as having five main features: inalienable, universal, indivisible, inabrogable and interdependent.

Inalienable

Human rights cannot be taken or given away.

Universal

Human rights apply to everyone, at all times, around the world.

Indivisible

All human rights are equally essential. One right is not more important than another.

Inabrogable

Human rights cannot be given up or traded for something else.

Interdependent

Human rights work together as a group. A single right cannot be effective on its own.
