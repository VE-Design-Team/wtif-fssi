Knowing your rights

Within the workplace, support workers and their organisation are accountable for the rights of service users and of each other. Accountability means observing, monitoring and reporting human rights breaches.

Select the image to learn more about the rights of the service users, support workers and organisation.

Service users should be aware that the organisation has policies and procedures to resolve a breach. If the organisation’s response is unsatisfactory, service users can go to external authorities (e.g. government agencies) and even to court.

Support workers need to know the organisation’s policies and procedures for lodging complaints. They should ensure that service users know they have the right to complain and support them to make their complaints heard.

Organisations should be transparent about their policies and procedures by clearly communicating how their staff handle matters affecting service users.
