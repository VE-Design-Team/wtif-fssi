Statutory authorities

In Australia, a formal human rights complaint is made to one or more statutory authorities. Statutory authorities are federal or state organisations responsible for enacting and upholding laws. Complaints about aged care are handled by federal statutory authorities, like the Aged Care Commissioner, whereas complaints about disability services involve state statutory authorities, like the Victorian Ombudsman.

Flip the cards to learn more about the different statutory authorities and the complaints they can take.

Victorian Equal Opportunity and Human Rights Commission (VEOHRC)

Investigates complaints involving discrimination or harassment in Victoria.

Department of Health and Human Services (DHHS)

Investigates complaints about disability services regulated by DHHS.

Disability Services Commissioner (DSC)

Investigates complaints about the delivery of disability services by their provider in Victoria.

NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission

Investigates complaints about disability services provided by NDIS.

Office of the Public Advocate (OPA)

Advocates for people with a disability, especially in cases of neglect, abuse and mistreatment.

Victorian Ombudsman

Investigates complaints about administrative actions by Victorian government agencies (e.g. Department of Human Services), local government and most statutory authorities (e.g. Vic Roads).

Victorian Civil and Administrative Tribunal (VCAT)

Investigates complaints from service users about unsatisfactory services from their providers.

Aged Care Commissioner

Investigates complaints about a service provider’s duties under the Aged Care Act 1997.
