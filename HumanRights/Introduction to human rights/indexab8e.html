Human rights based approaches

Human rights based approaches (HRBA) are about putting human rights theory into action. For a service provider, this means turning human rights laws into workplace policies and procedures. HRBA may vary from organisation to organisation, but they all follow the same five principles: participation, accountability, non-discrimination and equality, empowerment and legality. These five principles make up the PANEL model.

Participation

Everyone has the right to take part in making decisions that impact their human rights. Participation must be active, free and meaningful.

Accountability

Effective monitoring and problem-solving through laws, policies and procedures are needed. This is to make sure that human rights standards are being met and goals are achieved.

Non-discrimination and equality

All forms of discrimination must be prohibited, prevented and eliminated. Priority should be given to people in the most vulnerable situations.

Empowerment

Everyone has the right to claim and exercise their rights and freedoms. People need to be informed about their rights so they can make decisions and help develop policies and procedures that affect their human rights.

Legality

For a Human Rights Based Approach to be effective, there must be laws that recognise and enforce human rights.
