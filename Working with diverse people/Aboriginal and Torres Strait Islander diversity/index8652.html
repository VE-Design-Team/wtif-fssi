Regional terms and locations

Before 1788, Aboriginal and Torres Strait Island people identified themselves in local or regional terms. Today, many Aboriginal and Torres Strait Island people still prefer to identify themselves by these regional terms in their regional language. For example, the names of specific islands are preferred by many Torres Strait Islanders.

The following checklist outlines some of these regional terms and the approximate area of Australia a group comes from. If you are going to use these terms, please understand and respect their regional context. If you are not sure of the correct term to use, consult with a local Aboriginal person or your local Aboriginal community about their appropriateness.

Select each check box to learn some of the regional terms and the group of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Island people they refer to.
  • Goori – Refers to Aboriginal people from the north coast of New South Wales.
  • Palawa – Refers to Aboriginal people from Tasmania.
  • Anangu – Refers to Aboriginal people from Central Australia.
  • Noongar (or alternatively spelled Nyoongah) – Refers to Aboriginal people from the south-west corner of Western Australia.
  • Torres Strait Islander Peoples – Refers to the Indigenous people of the Torres Strait Islands, Queensland.
  • Nunga – Refers to Aboriginal people from southern South Australia.
  • Yolngu – Refers to Aboriginal people from north-eastern Arnhem Land in the Northern Territory.
  • Ngarrindjeri – Refers to Aboriginal people from the lower Murray River area and the Coorong and Lakes regions of South Australia.
  • Murri – Refers to Aboriginal people from Queensland and north-west New South Wales. Torres Strait Islander people do not identify with the term ‘Murri’.
  • Koori or Koorie – Refers to Aboriginal people from New South Wales or Victoria.
