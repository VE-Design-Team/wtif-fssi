Understanding legal context

Some aspects of diversity have been subject to laws at Commonwealth and state and territory level, which aim to prevent discrimination. Practitioners need to have a detailed understanding of the policies and procedures that surround their work. They also need to understand that many of these policies are responses to legislation.

Select each of the headings below to learn more about key federal and state legislation relating to discrimination

Human rights

The Victorian Charter of Human Rights and Responsibility Act, 2006 protects and promotes human rights, such as freedom of expression and beliefs, protection from degrading treatment and equality before the law. The Victorian Equal Opportunity Act, 1995 promotes anti-discrimination and equality in the workplace.

Workplace rights

The Fair Work Act, 2009 outlines the rights of all employees in Australia. The Act outlines 10 National Employment Standards that affect all Australians workers. Other rights are outlined in your Award or Enterprise Agreement. As a worker you have the right to join a union to support you to access your workplace rights. You can find the union that represents you through the Australian Council of Trade Unions.

Ageing rights

The Age Discrimination Act, 2004 prevents discrimination against older people.

The Aged Care Act, 1997 ensures government funding and accessibility of aged care services and provisions to individuals who need it.

Disability rights

The Disability Discrimination Act, 1992 and the Victorian Disability Act, 2006 highlight the rights of people with disabilities to the same privileges, freedoms and opportunities as everyone else.
