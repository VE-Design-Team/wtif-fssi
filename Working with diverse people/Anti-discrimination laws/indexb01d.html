The Australian Human Rights Commission

The Australian Human Rights Commission focuses on a range of groups and discrimination issues including age, asylum seekers and refugees, race, children’s rights, disability rights, sexual orientation, gender identity and intersex status.

Although non-discriminatory practice is a moral obligation that is grounded in humanist ideas of respect for people and a commitment to human rights, it is also a legal obligation. As a first generation human right, non-discrimination is enforceable by state, national and international laws. Support workers need to know which jurisdiction or area of authority handles discrimination.

Different geographical areas may involve different authorities, regulators and courts. These agencies may offer various services, including advocacy, mediation, civil remedy and even criminal prosecution. Broadly, Australian (Commonwealth) Acts of Parliament protect the rights of older people.

Until recently, Victorian (state) Acts protected the rights of people with disabilities. Now people with disabilities are also protected by national laws under the National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS) that has rolled out across Australia. The Victorian Equal Opportunity Human Rights Commission (VEOHRC) lists all Australian and Victorian Acts of Parliament that support anti-discrimination.
