The above principles are embodied in PANEL, a Human Rights based approach to service provision that is endorsed by the United Nations.

P-A-N-E-L stands for:

  • Participation
  • Accountability
  • Non-discrimination
  • Empowerment
  • Legality

Services should be welcoming, ensuring they are accessible for all people, physically and psychologically. Services must be responsive to communication and language needs (e.g., assistive technology and Auslan or language interpreters). Toilets and waiting rooms should be fully accessible (e.g., are spatially appropriate and have ramps). The setting of all facilities should be culturally appropriate to avoid offense to any individual or community.
