Personal, cultural, structural analysis

Applying the PCS analysis to ageism we would find:

  • Personal – physical and verbal abuse of older people. Talking down to and patronising an older person.
  • Cultural – assuming that all old people have cognitive impairment. Stereotyping and mocking older people.
  • Structural – thousands of Australian older people are waiting for home care packages to allow them to live at home with dignity. Conditions within some Australian aged care residential services are so poor that they are the subject of a Royal Commission. These problems are caused by under funding of services to support older Australians over many years. 

Identifying these three areas of discrimination in an aged care facility can highlight areas of education, staff training, and facility policies and practices that need be improved. It can also identify areas for advocacy.
