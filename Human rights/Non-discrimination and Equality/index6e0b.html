Identifying discrimination

Support workers need to be alert to any form of discrimination to prevent and respond to it appropriately in the workplace. People could be discriminated based on their:

  • disability
  • age
  • gender
  • culture or ethnic background
  • religion
  • sexuality.

Question 1 of 3

Is the following statement true or false? A residential facility only allows heterosexual couples to occupy a double suite. This is discrimination.



Correct

Discrimination can sometimes be hard to recognise. It may be based on outdated ideas or beliefs that are no longer true or acceptable. Speaking up and asking questions help identify and fight discrimination. If this matter is questioned by a resident, their relatives, or staff, the facility is accountable and needs to respond.

Incorrect

You may have a personal set of beliefs about sexuality, but you have no right to discriminate against others on the basis of sexuality.

Question 2 of 3

Is the following statement true or false? A trip is arranged for residents in a residential unit. The facility bus cannot accommodate the one user in a wheelchair. An accessible vehicle could have been booked but the organiser felt this would have slowed the trip down and cost more. This is discrimination.



Correct

Workers need to examine their own opinions of service users to ensure their actions are not prejudiced. The organiser’s assumption that including the resident with a wheelchair would slow things down and make the trip more expensive and less enjoyable is discriminating the resident based on their disability.
