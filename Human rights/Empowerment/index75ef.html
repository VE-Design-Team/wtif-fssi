Practice empowerment

Consider the two components of empowerment, awareness, autonomy and communication, in the following scenarios.

Question 1 of 2

Sarah is a resident in an aged care facility. She tends to wander into other people’s rooms without asking. Even though she doesn’t pose a safety risk, other residents have started to complain. They wish to have their privacy and worry that Sarah might take their belongings when they are not in their room. You have spoken with Sarah about respecting other people’s privacy and knocking before entering a room, however, Sarah has dementia and doesn’t always remember. Which is the most appropriate response?




Correct

Yes. Working together to keep an eye on Sarah would address the other residents’ complaints without mistreating Sarah. It could also lead to a better long-term solution if any particular patterns or behaviours are identified that could solve Sarah’s wandering.

Incorrect

No. Physically restraining or isolating Sarah in her room would be a human rights breach as it takes away her freedom of movement. The use of restraints is strictly regulated by the Senior Practitioner in the DHHS.

Incorrect

No. Having cameras would intrude on Sarah's rights to respect, dignity, and privacy, and also the rights of the other residents and staff.

Question 2 of 2

Martin suffers from depression and anxiety. His doctor has prescribed him some new medication. Martin does not like taking pills but has followed his doctor’s prescription. Martin tells you that he does feel less depressed and anxious on the medication, but that it also makes him feel foggy and he finds it hard to concentrate on tasks. He thinks that the medication is helping but he doesn’t like taking it. Which is the most appropriate response?




Correct

Yes. Martin has a right to participate in deciding his treatment. He should tell his doctor how he feels about taking medication and what affect it has on him. This will help Martin and his doctor find a treatment that he is happy with and that doesn’t negatively impact his abilities or quality of life.

Incorrect

No. Just because the doctor is a medical expert does not make her more important than Martin, especially when it comes to Martin’s care. Martin should be encouraged to voice his concerns, ask questions, and give his doctor a better understanding of his background and situation so they can find the best treatment options for him.

Incorrect

No. Martin has the right and freedom to choose how he wishes to live. If he is unhappy with the medication in any way, he should be encouraged to speak with his doctor to find out if there are other treatment option that he would be happier with.
