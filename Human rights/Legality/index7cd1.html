Being a human rights defender

As a support worker, you don’t need to know everything to defend the human rights of the people you support. Some things you should do are:

  • Listen to and observe the people you support.
  • Ask yourself how you would feel in the same situation.
  • Follow the policies of the organisation.
  • Ask for help from your supervisor first and failing that, from an advocacy organisation, from a regulator or from your union. 

In Victoria, the Department of Health and Human Services (DHHS) states that,

“People with disability have the same rights as other people in their community. They have the rights to freedom, respect, equality, and dignity, to fulfil their potential, exercise control over their own lives and live free from abuse and neglect.”

The same principles apply to people who are ageing. You can remember this as FRED – Freedom, Respect, Equality, and Dignity. As a support worker, you should continually ask yourself: am I supporting the people I work with to be Free, to be Respected, to be treated Equally and with Dignity (FRED), just like any other member of the community.
