Informal and formal complaints

Most breaches of human rights are resolved without formal complaints or actions. Not all human rights are legally enforceable and most breaches can be resolved through communication, advocacy, mediation and conflict resolution.

In an organisation where everyone is committed to a human rights-based approach, human rights will be the subject of constant discussion and will be resolved informally in most cases. Only when these informal attempts fail should a formal complaint be made.

There will be some situations in which legal action is the only thing left to protect or defend a person’s human rights. Your supervisor will be able to advise you about what to do in situations in which this approach is required.

Select each of the check boxes to learn about the different steps to making a complaint.
  • A service user makes an informal complaint to a support worker.
  • A complaint is made to the supervisor in the organisation.
  • A mediation process involving advocates for the service user and the organisation may be set up.
  • An external complaint can be made to the relevant statutory authority (e.g. the Disability Services Commissioner).
