Three key frameworks for human rights

The Victorian Department of Health and Human Services (DHHS) states that all people with a disability have the right to freedom, respect, equality and dignity (FRED). They have the right to exercise control over their own lives and to live free from abuse and neglect. Their rights are protected by three key frameworks:

  • The Victorian Charter of Human Rights and Responsibilities, 2006, protects the rights and freedoms and all people in the state of Victoria.
  • The UN Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD), 2007, details how the rights of people with disabilities should be upheld in all areas of life, including work, education, health services, transportation, justice and accessibility of the physical and social environment.
  • The Victorian Disability Act, 2006, states that community service organisations must be registered as disability service providers and keep to human services standards based on principles of empowerment, well-being, access and engagement and participation.

Accountability under these frameworks come in several forms that support workers need to know.

Disability Services Commissioner (DSC)

Works with people with disability and their service providers to resolve complaints.

The National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS) Quality and Safeguards Commission

Supports NDIS participants to exercise choice and control over their lives, ensures safeguards are in place for NDIS support and sets expectations for service providers and their staff to provide quality support.

Victorian Civil and Administrative Tribunal (VCAT)

Reviews, monitors and overrides inappropriate decisions or behaviours of service providers and their staff.

The Senior Practitioner in DHHS

Regulates and ensures best practice of restrictive interventions (i.e., bodily restraints and isolation) and compulsory treatment by service providers.
