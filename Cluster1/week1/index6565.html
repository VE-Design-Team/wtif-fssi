Seeking clarification

Sometimes it can be difficult to understand what someone else is saying. You may also find that you have trouble understanding information you read. When this happens, you must seek clarification.

Clarification allows you to check that you understand the situation. It is then that you can ask questions if you are confused or feel that you have misunderstood what is being said. It also tells the person you’re communicating with that you are interested in what they’re saying and that you want to understand them better. There are two main ways to clarify information and check for understanding:

  • by asking questions, to better understand what is being said or asked of you
  • by reflecting or summarising information back to a person verbally or in a written communication form to make sure important information is getting across.

Asking for clarification shows that you care about what the person is communicating. It ensures that what you are hearing is the same as what they are trying to say. Never assume that you know what a person is thinking or feeling. Ask open and closed-ended questions to discover what might really be happening with the resident to get as much information as possible.

Select the methods for clarifying information to find out more about it.
  • Reflecting
  • Summarising

Reflecting is like holding up a mirror to what another person is saying. It involves listening for the basic message in a conversation and repeating it back to the speaker in simple terms, paying attention to both their words and nonverbal cues. It shows the person that you understood their message or that they need to clarify further and provides an opportunity for them to correct any misunderstandings or confusion.

Summarising is a more formal way of showing that you understand a message. It involves repeating back to a person an overview of the main points covered in the conversation.

Summarising can be especially useful when giving or receiving instructions.

Summarising a conversation shows that you are listening and that you are attempting to understand a person properly. It also gives the other person an opportunity to clarify things you might not fully understand or are confused about. If further clarification is required after the conversation, make sure you chat to the person or make another time to discuss.
