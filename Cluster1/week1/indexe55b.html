Types of questions

Asking questions is an important part of communication. Questions allow you to find the information you need. There are two general question types.

Select each type of question to find out more about it.
  • Open-ended questions
  • Closed-ended questions

Open-ended questions usually begin with words such as ‘why’, ‘how’, or ‘tell me about’. They usually require a longer and more detailed answer. For example, ‘Why do you think you’re feeling frustrated today?’, or ‘Tell me a little about the job you’d like me to do.’

Open-ended questions encourage people to think and reflect before answering. They can be used to:

  • encourage people to voice their opinions and feelings
  • start conversations
  • find out about a person’s wishes, needs, and problems
  • test that a person understands what is being said by having them explain things back to you in their own words.

Closed-ended questions can be answered with a straight yes/no, or a simple word. For example, ‘Are you feeling frustrated?’, or ‘How long will the job take?’ To answer these questions the person can say yes or no (to feeling frustrated), or give a one-word answer like ‘10 minutes’ (for how long the job will take).

You can use closed-ended questions to obtain quick, factual responses. They are useful when time is limited or when the person might not be able to give more than a yes or no answer. Be aware though, closed-ended questions can limit conversation and prevent a person from telling you what they really think, feel or want. In fact, asking a series of closed-ended questions will often be less informative than asking one good, open-ended question that promotes a deeper conversation.
