Common issues with the integumentary system

Select each of the headings below to learn more about some of the common issues with the integumentary system.

Skin Cancer

Skin cancer is an abnormal change in the skin cells, caused by exposure to UV rays through sunlight, tanning booths and x-rays. The lighter a personâ€™s skin, the more susceptible they are to this form of cancer. People who are fair-skinned and redheaded are at the highest risk of skin cancer as their skin contains less protective melanin.

There are two main types of skin cancer: melanoma and non-melanoma. Non-melanomas are rarely life-threatening; however, melanomas can be aggressive, fast-spreading cancers than can cause death if left untreated.

Warts

Warts are small growths most often found on the hands and feet. They are caused by the human papillomavirus (HPV), and are more common in children, teens and other people with underdeveloped or weakened immune systems that are less able to resist the virus.

Eczema/Dermatitis

Eczema, or dermatitis, is an inflammation of the skin that makes it red, scaly and itchy. It is a genetic condition in which the skin barrier is unable to repair itself, allowing moisture out and irritants in to the body.

The entry of foreign irritants into the epidermal layer activates the immune system, which responds by becoming inflamed.

Acne

Acne is caused by an inflammation of the hair follicles or a blockage in the oil glands. If an oil gland or hair follicle becomes blocked, bacteria can multiply in it and cause redness and swelling.

Acne mainly affects the face, back and chest. It is most common in teenagers and young adults, and is usually a by-product of hormonal changes in puberty and an increase in the size and oil-production of the glands.
