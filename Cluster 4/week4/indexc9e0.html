The basic components of the human body

The human body is made up of a number of components and systems that function both separately and together to give us life. The basic building blocks of the human body are microscopic units called cells. Cells group together to form tissues, which then group together to form body parts and organs. Body parts and organs group together to form entire organisms, such as humans. In this section, we’ll look at these basic components of the human body:

  • cells
  • tissues
  • organs.
Select each of the building blocks of the human body to find out more about it.
Cell

A cell is the most basic structure in the human body. You can only see a cell under a microscope. Cells take oxygen and sugar and metabolise (eat) it to create energy, stay alive and function.

They also take other nutrients like protein and fat to repair themselves when damaged, and to produce chemicals they release and share with the whole body.

Tissues

Tissues are groups of cells of the same type. Cells within tissue look very similar to each other under a microscope and serve a specific function within an organ.

Tissue contains water, called extracellular fluid, which contains oxygen and nutrients that come from the blood, metabolic waste, carbon dioxide and products that the cell makes and shares with other body tissues, organs and systems.

Organs

Organs are structures made up of different tissues. Each tissue will have a specific role but they all combine as a team to deliver the function of the organ. In this visual example, the heart is an organ primarily made up of muscle tissue.
