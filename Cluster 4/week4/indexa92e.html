Common issues with the respiratory system

The following are some common injuries, diseases and other issues that affect the respiratory system.

Select each of the headings below to learn more about some of the common issues with the respiratory system.

Asthma

Asthma is a chronic disease involving the airways. It can affect the airways in one of three ways, each of which makes it difficult to breathe:

  • inflammation, which reduces the amount of air that can travel through the airways
  • constriction, in which muscles in the wall of the airway become tight and narrow
  • mucus production, in which mucus blocks the airways.

Common symptoms of asthma are wheezing, coughing, shortness of breath, and tightness in the chest. It can be exacerbated by exercise, or air that is polluted with particles such as mould, chemicals, gases, and cigarette smoke. Australia has the highest prevalence of asthma of all other countries, with around 10% of Australians suffering from some degree of asthma.

Influenza

Influenza, or ‘flu’, is a highly contagious viral infection, whose impact can range from mild to severe. It is spread by sneezing and coughing, hand-to-mouth contact, or via contaminated surfaces. Symptoms include fever and chills, coughing, sore throat, runny nose, stuffiness, aches and pains, fatigue and headache. As influenza is viral, it cannot be treated by antibiotics.

Hay fever

Hay fever is an allergic response to things like pollen, dust, fungus, and animal hair. It is caused by an overreaction of the immune system to normally harmless substances. The immune system responds by releasing histamine that causes swelling and inflammation. Hay fever is often seasonal. Symptoms include sneezing, runny nose, watery eyes, itching, and welts.

Bronchitis

Bronchitis is an inflammation of the bronchi, the tubes that carry air to the lungs. It can be caused by bacteria, pollutants such as smoke, or the same viruses that cause colds and influenza. In fact, bronchitis is a complication that commonly follows a particularly bad cold or flu. The symptoms of bronchitis are largely the same as influenza, but without the stuffiness and runny nose. Those at greater risk of developing bronchitis are the elderly, smokers, and those with poor immunity or a lung condition (like asthma).

Emphysema

Emphysema is a disease of the lungs, in which alveoli (the capillaries that transmit oxygen to the bloodstream) are impaired or destroyed. It is caused largely by cigarette smoking, which destroys lung tissue and causes inflammation (and subsequent obstruction) of the airways. It is also caused by long-term exposure to environmental pollutants and dust. Symptoms include shortness of breath, coughing and wheezing, fatigue, cyanosis, and decreased tolerance for physical activity.

Cancer

There are several cancers that affect organs in the respiratory system, including cancers of the mouth, trachea, and lungs.

  • Oral cancers occur in mouth parts such as the lips, tongue, and tonsils. The main causes of mouth cancers are cigarette smoking and alcohol consumption.
  • Lung cancer is the most common cause of cancer death in Australia. It can result from exposure to cigarette smoke, asbestos, certain chemical substances, or a family history of the disease.

Symptoms of respiratory cancers often include coughing, breathlessness, difficulty swallowing, recurrent chest infections, coughing up blood, unexplained weight loss, fatigue or loss of appetite.

Pulmonary oedema

Pulmonary oedema is a build-up of fluid in the lungs that causes shortness of breath. It can have a variety of causes, from heart disease, through to pneumonia, injury, and exposure to high altitudes. Other symptoms of pulmonary oedema include coughing, chest pain and fatigue.

Laryngitis

Laryngitis is an inflammation of the larynx, or voice box. It can be caused by things like a respiratory infection, cigarette smoking, pollution, or overuse of the vocal chords. Symptoms of laryngitis include hoarseness, discomfort, and temporary voice loss.
