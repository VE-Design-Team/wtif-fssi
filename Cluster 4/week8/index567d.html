Common issues with the immune system

Select each of the headings below to learn more about some of the common issues with the immune system.

Immunodeficiency and Autoimmunity

Immunodeficiency and autoimmunity are disorders at opposite sides of the immunity spectrum. 

Immunodeficiency is the result of a weak immune system that is incapable of properly fighting infection or disease. Immunodeficiency makes a person less resistant to invading viruses and bacteria and less able to combat foreign material when it enters the body.

Autoimmunity lies at the opposite end of the spectrum, and involves a hyperactive immune system that overreacts to harmless substances and the person’s own body tissues as if they were harmful foreign bodies. Common autoimmune diseases include rheumatoid arthritis, ulcerative colitis, and coeliac disease.

HIV/Aids

HIV (human immunodeficiency virus) is an acquired virus that can be transmitted via blood, semen or vaginal fluid. Once contracted, it weakens the immune system and reduces the body’s natural ability to fight infection.

If left untreated, HIV can lead to AIDS (acquired immune deficiency syndrome), a condition in which the immune system is highly compromised leaving the body susceptible to serious infection and death.

Asthma and Allergies

Asthma and allergies occur when the immune system overreacts to a normally harmless material like food particles, pollen, dust mites, pet hair or insect bites. The immune system mistakes the substance as a serious threat and produces antibodies designed the attack the allergen.

This can result in mild symptoms such as itching, runny nose, watery eyes and wheezing or life-threatening reactions like anaphylaxis. During anaphylaxis, body tissues swell and blood pressure can plummet, causing obstruction of the airways and hypotension.
