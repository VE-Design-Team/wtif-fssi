Common issues with the digestive system

The following are some common injuries, diseases and other issues that affect the digestive system.

Select each of the headings below to learn more about some of the common issues with the digestive system.

Cancer

Collectively, cancers of the digestive system are referred to as ‘gastrointestinal cancers’. There are several common cancers that affect organs in the digestive system, including cancers of the bowel, stomach, pancreas, and liver.

  • Bowel cancer affects the intestines, and is the second most common form of cancer in Australia. It is more prevalent in those over 50 years of age.
  • Liver cancer can result from hepatitis B or C, or chronic alcoholism.

Symptoms of a gastrointestinal cancer often include difficulty swallowing or defecating; abnormal bleeding; or nutrient deficiencies from the incomplete digestion of food.

Cirrhosis

Cirrhosis is a hardening and deformation of liver tissue that results in damage and impaired function. Common causes of cirrhosis include excessive alcohol consumption, hepatitis B and C, and fatty liver disease. Symptoms of cirrhosis include fatigue, nausea, abdominal pain and itching.

Soiling and Stool Holding (Encopresis)

Soiling and stool holding (known as encopresis) is a problem usually found in children. It occurs when a child resists the natural urge to defecate (stool holding), causing damage that allows stools to eventually leak out (soiling).

Gallstones

Small, crystallised masses of bile, called gallstones, can form in the gallbladder and grow larger over time. Most gallstones are harmless, but if they leave the gallbladder and block the bile ducts they can produce pain, inflammation, and damage to the pancreas and liver. Problems caused by gallstones are often treated by removing the gallbladder altogether.

Gastroenteritis

Gastroenteritis causes inflammation of the stomach lining and intestines. It occurs when harmful bacteria, parasites, or viruses are transmitted via bodily contact, or ingested through contaminated food or water. The most common type is viral gastroenteritis. Symptoms of gastro include abdominal pain, nausea and vomiting, diarrhoea, and fever.

Hepatitis

Hepatitis is an inflammation of the liver, which reduces its ability to function. There are three types of hepatitis:

  • Hepatitis A is transmitted by faeces through infected food or water, such as raw shellfish.
  • Hepatitis B is transmitted through bodily fluids such as blood, semen and vaginal fluid.
  • Hepatitis C is transmitted via infected needles used in intravenous drug use, sexual contact, childbirth, or sharing objects like razors.

Symptoms of hepatitis include fatigue, loss of appetite, abdominal pain, odd-coloured urine or stools, sore joints, and jaundice.

Jaundice

Jaundice is a yellowing of the skin and whites of the eyes. Jaundice is not a disease or health condition in itself, but it can be a sign that there is a problem in the digestive system, primarily the liver, gallbladder or pancreas.

Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS)

Irritable bowel syndrome, or IBS, is the most common digestive disorder, affecting a large number of people. Sufferers experience abdominal pain and cramping, flatulence, bloating, and constipation or diarrhea. There is no cure and the exact cause of IBS is unknown. However, it can exacerbated by certain foods along with emotional stress.

Pancreatitis

Pancreatitis is an inflammation of the pancreas, and can be chronic or short-lived. Chronic pancreatitis can cause scarring, tissue damage and loss of function. The most common causes of pancreatitis are gallstones and chronic alcoholism. Other causes include medications, smoking, and some infectious diseases.
