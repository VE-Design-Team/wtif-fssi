Endocrine system

The endocrine system is made up of organs called glands. Glands release chemicals called hormones into the blood stream. These hormones travel around the body (carried in blood) to the areas of the body that need it to function properly. Some of the endocrine glands we will cover next are actual organs that you have learnt about in previous systems. We will revisit them again to explain their endocrine function.

Parts of the endocrine system

Select the + to learn more about the different parts that make up the nervous system.
  • Hypothalamus and Pituitary: The hypothalamus is responsible for homeostasis, which is the body’s tendency to maintain a stable condition that is optimal for survival. As such, it regulates things like body temperature, hunger and thirst, sex drive, moods and sleep. The pituitary gland (sometimes called the ‘master gland’) controls several other glands in the body, including the thyroid, adrenal glands, ovaries and testicles. It produces hormones critical to growth, reproductive processes and lactation.
  • Pineal Gland: The pineal gland produces the hormone melatonin, which controls sleep patterns and responds to dark and light.
  • Parathyroid Glands: The four parathyroid glands regulate the body’s calcium levels and bone health.
  • Thyroid Gland: The thyroid gland controls a number of hormonal processes, including metabolism, heart rate, sexual function, and the rate at which a person grows and develops. An overactive thyroid will speed up bodily function, and an underactive thyroid will slow it down.
  • Thymus Gland: The thymus gland is also a major part of the lymphatic system, and works with the immune system to fight infection and disease. It transforms white blood cells into T cells, and releases these cells into nearby lymph nodes.
  • Heart: The heart also functions as part of the endocrine system, producing hormones that control blood volume and blood pressure by working with the kidneys to regulate the amount of water in the blood.
  • Adrenal Glands: The adrenal glands secrete hormones that control blood pressure, metabolism, sex drive and stress responses. They make adrenaline, which is important to the body’s ‘fight or flight’ response when placed in stressful situations.
  • Pancreas: The pancreas helps the body to maintain healthy blood glucose levels. Glucose is a simple sugar that provides energy to cells. When blood glucose is high, the pancreas helps lower it by producing the hormone insulin. When levels are low, the pancreatic gland produces glucagon which causes the liver to release glucose.
  • Kidneys: The kidneys are part of both the urinary and endocrine systems. As an endocrine organ they work with the heart to release hormones related to blood pressure. They also influence the production of red blood cells and vitamin D.
  • Ovaries (Female): Ovaries produce and secrete the female sex hormones oestrogen, testosterone, and progesterone.
  • Testes (Male): Testes produce sperm, and secrete the male sex hormone testosterone.
