Common issues with the male reproductive system

Select each of the headings below to learn more about some of the common issues with this system.

Erectile Dysfunction

Erectile dysfunction is the inability to achieve or maintain an erection. It is not a disease in itself, but is a symptom of an underlying physical or psychological problem.

Erectile dysfunction can be caused by physical conditions such as diabetes, hypertension, obesity or smoking, or by psychological factors such as stress, anxiety, or depression. The vast majority of erectile dysfunction is due to physical illness.

Prostate Enlargement

Prostate enlargement happens naturally as a result of ageing, usually after 40 years of age. Many men with an enlarged prostate experience problems with urination, and it is the most common health problem in men over 60 years of age.

Symptoms include a weak stream of urine; dribbling or leaking of urine; inability to empty the bladder; a frequent, strong urge to urinate; and blood in the urine.

Cancers of the Male Reproductive System

Prostate cancer is the most commonly diagnosed cancer in Australian men, and the third leading cause of cancer death. It results from the abnormal development of cells in the prostate, and can be caused by diet, heredity factors, high testosterone levels, or advancing age.

Symptoms are similar for prostate enlargement, and can include unexplained pain, weight loss and fatigue. Testicular cancer is common in younger men aged 18 to 39. The most common symptom is a painful lump or swelling in one of the testicles. Most testicular cancers are successfully treated if detected.

Infertility

Male infertility is the inability to impregnate a fertile female. The most common causes of male infertility are:

  • obstructions that prevent sperm from travelling or leaving the penis
  • low sperm quality or reduced numbers of sperm
  • functional problems such as impotence or ejaculation issues
  • hormonal problems that affect sperm production and fertility.
