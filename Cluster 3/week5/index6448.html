Positive ageing

Research by the Australian Psychologial Society (APS) notes that positive ageing is a response to a common misconception that older people are a burden, portrayed as dependent recipients of government benefits, heavy users of health care services, and unemployed. Positive ageing recognises that older people make many important contributions to society. Many are informal carers and volunteers, and are the core foundation to many families. The idea of positive ageing is not new. Many of the support approaches and theories you have learnt about and are beginning to put into practice are based on similar concepts of positive ageing. Positive ageing is directed at older people, and is an attitude to ageing that focus’ on the positives, what can be achieved to ensure their quality of life is enhanced and they feel empowered to set and achieve goals. Positive ageing suggests that older people should:

  • Stay connected with family and socially.
  • Eat a healthy diet.
  • Maintain a positive mindset, set goals and work towards them.
  • Have regular medical check-ups.
  • Keep mentally active.
  • Do regular physical activity.

 As a support worker, you have a crucial role in assisting residents in each of these areas of positive ageing.
