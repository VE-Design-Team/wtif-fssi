What is personal support?

As a support worker, you will be responsible for providing personal support to residents. Personal support includes enabling them to actively participate in all daily living activities to the best of their ability, encouraging them to make their own decisions and maximising their independence. Daily living activities include showering, toileting, eating meals, brushing teeth, dressing and grooming.

These are considered basic self-care tasks. Many of the residents you will work with will need support with these tasks because of frailty, illness or disability. Some residents may be able to perform these activities without any help or with only minimal assistance, while others will need your help all of the time. The key is to support the individual needs of all residents and assist them to be as independent as possible.

As a support worker, you have a legal and moral responsibility to keep residents safe from harm whilst they are in your care. This is called your ‘duty of care’. You should be aware of what is required of you in your role, and follow your organisation’s policies, protocols and procedures when dealing with equipment and providing care.

Safety

Safety

  • Maintaining a safe environment for residents.
  • Taking reasonable care to protect them from injury.

 

Privacy

Privacy

  • Respecting the privacy of residents at all times.
  • Providing residents with privacy.
  • Keeping all material confidential, and safely storing and disposing of all personal information and records relating to residents, staff and others.

 

Dignity and respect

Dignity and respect

  • Considering the potential impact that your support will have on the resident.
  • Identifying and taking care to meet their physical, sensory, religious, spiritual and cultural needs.
  • Providing care with empathy and sensitivity, without exploitation, abuse, discrimination or harassment.
  • Respecting the resident’s right to choose the care they want and from whom they want it.
  • Respecting the resident’s right to refuse treatment.
  • Respecting the resident’s right to take risks in order to optimise their quality of life.
