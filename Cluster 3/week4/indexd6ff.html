Verbal & non-verbal communication

People with dementia are especially tuned in to body language, tone and pitch, which often convey your attitude. While they may not be able to understand what is being said, you must always remember they still have feelings and emotions. A compassionate, non-judgemental, and accepting approach is essential.

Select each of the following communication strategies to learn more.

Non-verbal communication

  • Approach slowly from front
  • Crouch at eye level
  • Make and maintain eye contact
  • Use reassuring facial gestures (i.e. smiling, nodding etc)
  • Use reassuring body language (i.e. gentle touches to keep their attention)

Verbal communication

  • Use the resident’s name
  • Use a language they understand
  • Use reassuring words, phrases
  • Use short and simple sentences
  • Speak slowly and allow plenty of time for a response
  • Turn off distractions (TV/radio)
  • Acknowledge the resident’s distress and allow them to express it
  • Accept their reality
  • Assist the resident with problem solving
  • Use a calm, soothing, empathetic tone of voice
  • Offer words when resident cannot remember the one to use
  • Use positive reminiscence (recollection)
  • Develop a good rapport
  • Provide praise
  • Use humour
  • Avoid authoritative language (don’t, can’t, must, should)
  • Don’t interrupt, criticise or argue.
