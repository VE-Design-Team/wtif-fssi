Monitoring the changing needs of residents

As a support worker, you have a special working relationship with your residents. You assist them with daily living activities, support them through recreational and respite activities, and spend most of your time at work being with them. Therefore, you are in the best position to observe and monitor your resident’s health, behaviour and care needs, and note any changes from their usual state.

A resident’s individualised care plan lists their care requirements, behavioural tendencies, lifestyle habits and current health condition, including diagnosed illnesses and prescribed medication. This individualised care plan is an important document that informs staff at the facility how to best care for the resident. It is a legal document and must be current and relevant to the resident’s changing needs. Let’s look at an example.

Whilst Frank O’Connell has osteoarthritis and some joint stiffness, he values his independence and preserves his dignity by carrying out his daily living activities unaided. Most recently though, he has been struggling to scoop his food out of the plate and into his mouth, wincing in pain every time he has to bend his wrist to eat. Have a look at the eating and drinking section of Mr O’Connell’s old individualised care plan, and answer the questions that relate at the end of this page.

Question 1 of 2

Does the Individualised Care Plan reflect the wishes of Mr O’Connell to remain independent with some assistance as he eats?



Correct

The rights based approach to aged care guarantees the resident’s right to choose the level of care they want to receive. This aids their empowerment and is important for their physical and mental well-being.

Incorrect

Re-check the highlighted options on the individualised care plan and retry the question.

Question 2 of 2

Does the Individualised Care Plan reflect the current care needs of Mr. O’Connell as he eats?



Incorrect

Not at this time, Mr O’Connell is currently wincing in pain and spilling food on himself. The individualised care plan is not currently reflecting the care needs of Mr O’Connell. Retry this question.

Correct

The individualised care plan does not account for Mr O’Connell requiring assistance to consume his food. The current plan needs to be modified to better reflect the care needs of Mr O’Connell and, at the same time, respect his wishes to remain independent.
