The future of mobility aids

There are significant developments underway for mobility aids.

Select each of the headings to learn more about the future of AT for these devices. When you have finished, select again to close the section.

Wheelchairs

There are currently several options being explored for controlling powered wheelchairs, including:

  • a cloud-based voice recognition system
  • a combination of voice recognition and brain interface
  • a voice-controlled ‘smart’ powerchair
  • an internet-enabled powerchair that provides access to medical records, cognitive assistance (e.g. reminders around mealtimes, medicine and appointments) and obstacle avoidance and orientation and navigation mobility functions.

While these advances are still a long way from commercial production, they demonstrate that wheelchair technology is evolving.

Prosthetics/biomedical

Current advancements in mechanical augmentation for legs aim to help users who have difficulty in walking unaided. This will have great benefits for older people and those affected by stroke, multiple sclerosis, post-polio syndrome or muscular dystrophy.

Robots

Robots are advancing quickly.

Robear is an example of a robot designed to lift patients. Robots will replace traditional hoists in the near future. When they are introduced, robots will primarily be used in large organisations and facilities, as the costs will be too high for individual users.

Cars

The development of self-driving car technology is well underway and will eventually replace traditional human-steered cars, buses and trucks. This technology will have major advantages for the wider population, as people will no longer have to concentrate or remain sober to drive and accidents are likely to reduce. The implications for people who are currently unable to drive at all are even more significant.
