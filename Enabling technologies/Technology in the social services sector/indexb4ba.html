Enabling technologies in our lives

Enabling technologies are technologies that enhance access to information, communication, or the way we interact with our environment. Broadband internet, smart phones and glasses are a few examples.

Assistive technology (AT) is technology that specifically provides people with practical solutions to everyday life activities. They promote greater independence and safety in performing tasks that people may otherwise have difficulty with or be unable to accomplish without assistance. Assistive technology ranges from simple household items like a jar opener, to a car that helps you travel long distances quickly.

We all use assistive technology in our everyday lives, including lights to help us see in the dark, alarm clocks to help us wake up, and remote controls to adjust the television. Some ATs are designed to help bridge gaps in our capabilities, such as eye glasses and hearing aids.

This course focuses on the role of assistive technology (AT) in supporting older people and people with disability. While you do not necessarily need to know how to acquire and use all the technologies available, it is important for you to be familiar with them, as you will, no doubt, come across them in your work.

We will focus on the impact that assistive technology (AT) can have in transforming and building better lives, including the benefits and risks, challenges, your role in facilitating support user acceptance, selecting and supporting the use of positive technology, and staying up-to-date with advancing technology in your workplace.

A smartphone is a good example of AT. We use them to communicate, get directions, find information, alert us of reminders and much more. Most people rely on smartphones daily.

An escalator is an example of AT that helps us get from a lower floor to a higher floor without using stairs. Most of us would choose to take an escalator, especially to avoid a tiring or strenuous climb up many stairs.

A remote control is an AT device that helps us to adjust volume, channels or make selections without having to get out of our seats. Most of us use remote controls every day.

Robots are a form of AT for performing physical tasks and chores (e.g. cleaning, lifting, moving, etc.), their use is becoming more common. We will look at the use of robots in the support services sector later in this course.

More than half of the population requires glasses to see with clear vision. In terms of an AT that supports impairment, glasses are the most common for supporting visual impairment.
