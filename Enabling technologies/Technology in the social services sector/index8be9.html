The human factor

Support workers should not feel threatened by technology. Despite its ability to replace certain human functions, it cannot replace the human factor.

The application of technology in support services merely transforms the support worker’s role by increasing independence for older people and people with disability and reducing the requirement for functional support. This creates more time for support workers to deliver human care qualities such as empathy, emotional connection and meaningful communication.

One example of this is the use of robots to perform routine cleaning and hygiene chores. Support workers can spend less time on these tasks and more time engaging with or planning fun activities for the people they are supporting.
