Legislation, policy and procedures and ethics of care

Everyday you are faced with a variety of situations which require a different response. Many of these responses will be guided by the policy and procedures of your employer. Many of the policies and procedures in your workplace are supported by some legislation such as the Work Health and Safety Act.

It’s important that you understand and apply the policies and procedures in your workplace. If you don’t understand them, ask your supervisor for advice.  However, you will also find yourself in many situations where your actions are not controlled by rules and it is in this area where you can use ethics of care.

Being ethical is just as important as other areas, such as legislation and workplace policies and procedures, to ensure the quality of life of people who use aged care and disability support services.  

Flip the card to see the differences between these three areas which guide your actions.

Legislation
  • Work Health and Safety Act
  • Freedom of Speech
  • Equal Opportunity Act
  • Aged Care Act.

Policy and procedures
  • No drinking alcohol at work
  • Complete incident report form
  • Do not give home address to people who use your service
  • No overtime permitted on public holidays.

Ethics of care
  • Being attentive to someone’s needs
  • Being respectful of someone’s chosen lifestyle
  • Supporting trust in a relationship
  • Involving people in decisions about their care or support.
